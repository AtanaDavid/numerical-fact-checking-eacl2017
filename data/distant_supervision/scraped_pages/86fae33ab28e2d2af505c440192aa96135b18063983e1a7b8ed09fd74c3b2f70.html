Sunday, 14 May, 2000, 22:21 GMT 23:21 UK
Israel releases Hamas founder

An Israeli soldier on guard in Bethlehem
Israel has released from prison one of the founders of the radical Palestinian movement, Hamas, which has carried out a campaign of violence against the Middle East peace process.

Salah Shihadah was jailed 11 years ago. He completed an eight-year sentence but was then kept in detention without trial.

His release came on the third day of clashes between Israeli soldiers and hundreds of Palestinians demonstrating against the detention of Palestinian prisoners in Israeli jails.




Palestianians mourn Israel's creation

Reports say at least 20 Palestinians were wounded.

Palestinian hospital staff said most of the injured had been hit by rubber-coated bullets, but there were some wounds from live rounds.

In the West Bank, thousands of Palestinians held rallies to mark al-Nakba, or "the catastrophe" - the term used by Palestinians to describe the creation of the state of Israel 52 years ago.

Deadlock

One of the protest organisers, Khaled al-Khatib, said the rallies were an expression of popular frustration at the lack of progress in the peace talks.

The senior Palestinian negotiator, Saeb Erekat, and his Israeli counterpart, Oded Eran, held talks on Sunday over the release of 200 Palestinian prisoners.


A child injured during protests in Ramallah

They ended in deadlock, with Mr Erekat demanding that Israel free the prisoners immediately, and Mr Eran saying that street violence must cease before the prisoners could be released.

Many of the 1,600 Palestinians in Israeli jails have been on hunger strike since the beginning of May in a bid to highlight their case.
