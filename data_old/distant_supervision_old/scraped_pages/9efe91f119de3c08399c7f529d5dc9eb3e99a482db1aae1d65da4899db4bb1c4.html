Eightes pop legends the Thomson Twins on playing Live Aid.. with Madonna as a backing singer

  • 06:00, 10 Apr 2015
  • Updated 11:59, 10 Apr 2015
  • By Rick Fulton

At the time, Madonna was the hottest female star on the world thanks to Like a Virgin and was brought on stage by Nile Rodgers.

  •   Share

Get daily news by email

SHARING the stage with Madonna would be a big deal for most acts.

But when it happened to the Thompson Twins at the Live Aid gig in Philadelphia in 1985, frontman Tom Bailey admits it didn’t even register.

He laughed: “I was hardly aware of her contribution at the time.

“It was contribution of fame rather than notes.”

Back then she was the hottest female star on the planet thanks to second album Like a Virgin and was brought on stage by Nile Rodgers, who had produced the record and was working on the Thompson Twins’ next album.

At Live Aid, Madonna was on backing vocals and Nile on rhythm guitar for the Brit group’s cover of the Beatles’ Revolution.

The Thompson Twins – Tom, Alannah Currie and Joe Leeway – were big stars, heavily rotated by MTV. Their fourth album Into the Gap had gone to No1 in the UK and No10 in America.

And while Madonna is undisputably the queen of pop, the Chic legend is currently as hot as he was in the disco-flavoured 70s, thanks to his work with Daft Punk.

Tom said: “I think he’s the most fantastic success story. He started out as a success, became a miserable failure and came back with massive success.”

At the time it was reported Nile had been drafted in to help Tom, who had collapsed with nervous exhaustion, finish the Future Days album.

Tom said: “He was asked to work with us to save the project but that was to appeal to his sense of being the Red Adair of producers at the time.

“The album was substantially made by the time he got involved.

“He has talked about it but he had big problems when I worked with him with drink and drugs. But I loved working with him. I wanted to put more guitars on Thompson Twins music and I got one of the greatest rhythm guitarists ever working for us.”

And Tom would jump at the chance to work with him again.

He said: “I would love to. I’ve kept in contact with him and was texting him recently about getting together in New York during the Retro tour but it didn’t work out.”

The Retro Futura US tour was the start of Tom reconnecting with his 80s heyday and included Midge Ure and Howard Jones.

Both of them will also be on the bill for the Rewind Scotland festival at Scone Palace in July – back for its fifth consecutive year.

Other acts include Kim Wilde, Nik Kershaw and Belinda Carlisle.

Now a solo act, Tom tops the bill on the Saturday night and it’ll be the first time in 30 years Tom has played Scotland.

He said: “I’m fully aware my headline status is partly my rarity.If I keep doing it, my rarity will diminish. In this business you go pretty quickly from ‘Oh wow’ to ‘Oh no, not you again’.”

Tom turned his back on pop for most of the last two decades.

He did dub music as International Observer, indo-fusion as Holiwater and the multi-media Bailey-Salgado Project.

But after Mexican pop star Aleks Syntek asked him to sing on an album, he began warming to his old pop life and hits such as Hold Me Now, Doctor! Doctor! and You Take Me Up – which made the Thompson Twins stars in the 80s.

But first he had to buy his old music.

He said: “I didn’t listen to those songs for a long time. I had to go out and buy a CD of the greatest hits.

“I brought it home, played the intros to a few songs and within an hour more or less knew which ones I wanted to do and didn’t want to do.”

The Rewind audience will get the hits apart from We Are Detective because it was a duet with Alannah.

If You Were Here from the 1984 Molly Ringwald movie Sixteen Candles is likely to feature, even though it was never a single.

He said: “When I was asking people what I should play that one always gets picked, which is great because I love it too.”

With most of the 80s acts, it wasn’t just about the music. One sniper called the trio “the three haircuts” and they were known for their silk suits and pearls.

Tom said: “I haven’t thought about what I’ll be wearing yet but I do wear a fingerless glove on my right hand.

“The reason is I play the tambourine vigorously and I can really hurt my left hand.

“People see it and say to me ‘Wow fingerless gloves – that’s
so retro.’”

But while Tom and some of the 80s fashion will be at Rewind, former bandmates Alannah and Joe won’t.

He said: “Alannah was very against it at first and thought it was beneath everyone’s dignity.

“Joe left the band officially before we stopped touring. I invited him to the Retro show in Los Angeles last year because he lives near there but he didn’t show up.

“My explanation for them is that when the band stopped the two of them didn’t play another note of music.

“They weren’t really musicians. They are both creative individuals who found other ways of making trouble but for the past 30 years all I’ve done is make music.”

The group – named after the detective brothers in Tintin – were never a duo.

Formed in 1977, the line-up at one point boasted seven members but became a trio in 1982 with New Zealand percussionist Alannah, who would later become Tom’s wife, and Joe on congas.

Their mix of percussion and synths soon won them fans with Love On Your Side becoming the band’s first UK top 10 single in 1983.

Their third album Quick Step and Side Kick went to No2 in 1983 and the follow-up Into the Gap reached No1.

Tom’s long red ponytail, Joe’s dreadlocks and Alannah’s blonde bird’s nest of curls and shaved sides made them distinctive.

Tom said: “We did set ourselves up as being cartoonish. MTV wanted wacky videos. It gave us a foot in the door and we hoped people would like the music as well.”

After appearing on US Live Aid, Tom’s collapse from exhaustion saw the UK tour scrapped. Joe left the band in 1986 and Tom and Alannah continued making music but with less commercial success.

Tom admitted: “I became more experimental with the records and surprise, surprise, they didn’t sell as many because they were more challenging and darker.”

In 1991, a techno tune Come Inside was given to DJs under the name Feedback Max. It proved popular until it emerged it was a Thompson Twins tune and sales dropped.

“Our moment in the sun had gone,” Tom conceded.

In 1993 the duo became Babble making chilled-out dance music until 1996. Tom and Alannah, who have two children, divorced in 2003.

Tom is a big draw for Rewind as he hasn’t been part of the nostalgia circuit until recently.

He said: “It will be a passionate occasion, I think. Our songs were the soundtrack to some people’s youth.

“It’s more than just music to them.”

  • Rewind Scotland takes place at Scone Palace from July 24-26. For tickets and info go to www.rewindfestival.com or ticketmaster.co.uk .
