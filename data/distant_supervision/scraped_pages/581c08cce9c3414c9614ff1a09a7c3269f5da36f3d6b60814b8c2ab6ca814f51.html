Homicide, a Small and Decreasing Problem:

  Although media focus has recently been squarely on homicide and particularly “mass” shootings, the fact is that homicide is a small and decreasing problem for the United States. In 2011 homicide was one-half of one percent of total deaths.

  As shown in the graph below homicide reached a high in 1993 and has since been declining overall and in particular over the last 5 years.

  For a good discussion of why homicides have decreased I suggest the book Freakonomics by Steven Levitt and Stephen Dubner. To paraphrase, they determined that the main reason homicides decreased was a consequence of Roe Vs. Wade in 1973. To put it succinctly, because murder was disproportionately affecting the same demographic who chose abortion, the people who would have been doing the murdering weren’t there two decades later. This will be discussed further in a future section, Abortion and Guns, two things Republicans and Democrats Should Agree on.

Homicides, by the numbers

The majority of homicides (68%) in the United States in 2011 were committed with firearms, handguns especially. The popularity of handguns stems from ease of concealment. Walking down the street with a rifle or shotgun will quickly find you having a discussion with the local constabulary, most likely at gunpoint. Also after you shoot someone it is handy to slip the gun in your pocket and run away, this is difficult with a long-gun.

  I will take this opportunity to point out that the category ‘rifles’ includes “assault rifles” as well as bolt action, pump action, lever action, and every other kind of action type.  Because rifles (of all types) account for such a small number of murders, the FBI has not seen the necessity of making assault rifles their own category. This problem is complicated by the fact that multiple definitions of assault rifles exist so misreporting could be a serious issue. There are private sources which claim through polling and other methods to have estimates of assault rifle use in crime however I have chosen not to rely on them for methodological reasons.

  Sorting the data by percent we find that after handguns, knives are the next most preferred weapon, followed (for weapons of known type) by hands-feet-fists, and then blunt objects. Finally we get back to firearm-related deaths with shotguns (2.8%) and rifles (2.5%, including assault rifles).
  Although I will talk about this more explicitly in a future section on the Assault Weapons Ban (when I get around to it), the media has inspired the perception that assault rifles are a significant homicide tool, this is simply not the case; making the generous assumption that half of rifle deaths are some kind of “assault rifle” twice as many people are bludgeoned to death, four times as many beaten to death, eight times as many stabbed or slashed, and thirty times as many shot with handguns.

  People who suggest that renewing the AWB will reduce US mortality are simply mathematically inept, these weapons represent a minor contributor to a statistically insignificant cause of death.
