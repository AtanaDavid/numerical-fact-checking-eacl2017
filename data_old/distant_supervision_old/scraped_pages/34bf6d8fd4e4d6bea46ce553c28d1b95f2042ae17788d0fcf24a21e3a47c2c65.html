Windows 10 is now available to download in the UK

MICROSOFT has rolled out its hotly-anticipated new operating system.

By Aaron Brown Aaron Brown
PUBLISHED: 09:19, Wed, Jul 29, 2015 | UPDATED: 09:50, Wed, Jul 29, 2015

REUTERS

After months of tweaks, beta releases and clarifications over free upgrades – Windows 10 is here

After months of confusing clarifications about who was eligible for the free upgrade, beta software releases and some worrying security settings – Windows 10 is here.

The redesigned operating system – and Microsoft's last major Windows release – this morning launched across the UK and 189 other countries across the world.

Microsoft has staggered the roll-out, prioritising those who pre-registered to get access to the new operating system.

The new operating system will be the first to unite all Windows-powered devices – from sprawling desktop set-ups to svelte smartphones, multi-touch tablets, Raspberry Pis and even the firm's own Xbox One video game console.

Microsoft hopes Windows 10 will be installed on one billion devices worldwide within the first one to two years.

Windows 10 combines many features from the desktop-focused Windows 7 and the touch-centric Windows 8.

PA

Windows 10 is designed to combine the best elements of Windows 7 and Windows 8.1

PA

Cortana makes her desktop debut alongside a notification menu which sync across Windows devices

The much-loved Start menu – which was removed in the latter to a deafening public outcry – makes a return in the new OS.

However Microsoft has merged the traditional menu with the live tiles it debuted in its mobile operating system, Windows Phone.

The US technology firm hopes the changes will appeal to those upgrading from both Windows 7 and Windows 8, the two OS releases eligible for the free update.

The free upgrade offer, which will allow millions of PC users to switch to Windows 10, will expire next year.

Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella believes the firm's latest release should help people move from "needing Windows, to choosing Windows, to loving Windows."

This new message – hoping to dissociate the latest OS from the sluggish XP experience often found in the office or school – is reflected in the Redmond firm's first Windows 10 advertisements, which roll-out today.

Those who are not eligible for the free update will have to pay £99 for the Home version of Windows 10, which will go on sale at the end of the month.

The final retail packaging for the OS leaked online earlier this month.

Microsoft says there are currently 1.5 billion Windows users worldwide, but the software has come under increasing pressure in recent years from Apple's OS X, which runs on their range of Macintosh desk and laptops, and has seen sales steadily climb while the rest of the market – including Windows – has struggled in the age of mobile devices.

Having also demonstrated how holographic computing can work with Windows 10 via the firm's HoloLens augmented reality headset, Microsoft is hoping to move back to the cutting edge of the technology market with its new release.
