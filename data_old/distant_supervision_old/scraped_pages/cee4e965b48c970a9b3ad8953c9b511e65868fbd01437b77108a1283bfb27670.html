    As much as Simon Cowell stresses every year that it's only the winner of The X Factor who will walk away with that lucrative recording contract, finishing second sometimes turns out to be the best result.

    In fact, there are as many success stories amongst the runners-up as there are amongst the champions. But which loser ultimately scored the biggest win?

    We rank the post-X Factor music careers of all the second-placed stars the show hasn't crowned, below.

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    12. NICHOLAS MCDONALD

    ©  WENN

    Little Scottish charmer Nicholas McDonald narrowly missed out on the X Factor crown back in 2013, losing out to Sam Bailey. But within three months the singer-songwriter had recorded and released his debut album In the Arms of an Angel after bagging a deal with RCA. It peaked at number six on the UK album chart.

    His first single, however, gained very little traction. 'Answerphone' was co-written by Nicholas with Paul Barry (Lionel Richie, Celine Dion, Ronan Keating), but stalled at number 73 in the UK. The 19-year-old is still very much playing live shows though, as well as uploading covers to his social media channels. What's more, he also became godfather to Sam Bailey's daughter Miley.

    11. JAHMENE DOUGLAS

    Former Asda shop worker Jahmene Douglas finished behind James Arthur on The X Factor back in 2012. But the following summer he achieved a number one album in the UK with his debut album Love Never Fails. It included a huge duet with his mentor Nicole Scherzinger on 'The Greatest Love of All', as well as a special team-up with Stevie Wonder. Jahmene even got to sing his cover of 'Titanium' in a special international edition of The X Factor in the summer of 2013.

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    But despite the chart success, Jahmene parted ways with his major deal at Sony Music and is now signed to indie label Moonshot Music. He gained praise for his soulful song 'Down for Love' last November, but the single struggled to build momentum commercially. A second album is slated for later this year.

    10. MARCUS COLLINS

    ©  WENN

    Scouse favourite Marcus Collins finished second to Little Mix back in 2011, but he still managed to clinch himself a record deal after the show. He caused a bit of stir when he announced that his debut single would be a cover of The White Stripes' classic hit 'Seven Nation Army', but it managed a No.9 peak in the UK nonetheless.

    His self-titled debut album fared a little better, reaching No.7, but it wasn't enough to save his contract at Sony Music's RCA Records. Especially when his second single, 'Mercy', limped into the chart at No.194. Eek. Marcus turned his attention to the West End, though, and is currently enjoying a successful stint in Kinky Boots, as well as having toured with Hairspray. As for that second album: it's currently all still a bit TBC.

    9. REGGIE N BOLLIE

    ©  Syco

    Ghanian duo Reggie Zippy and Bollie Babeface (catchier than their real names of Ainooson and Hamid) charmed the whole nation with their dancehall vibes and infectious energy, but it ultimately wasn't enough to win over Louisa Johnson. It was enough to win over Simon Cowell though, and the duo signed to his Syco record label earlier this year.

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    Their debut single 'New Girl' shortly followed, brimming with bouncy beats and a jubilant chorus. It was released back in May 2016 and has peaked at No.26 in the UK so far. There's still time for it to climb higher, as well as the promise of a full album later this year.

    8. ANDY ABRAHAM

    ©  REX/Ken McKay

    Former bin man Andy Abraham will probably go down as the closest ever runner-up to winning The X Factor. He was pipped to the post by Shayne Ward back in 2005 by a very narrow 1.2% of the vote. But Andy still went on to release his debut album, which peaked at number two and sold just over 300,000 copies. A second collection, titled Soul Man, followed, and believe it or not, a Very Best Of collection was released in 2008.

    The latter coincided with his disastrous placing at Eurovision for the UK, finishing last with 14 points for 'Even If'. Since then, however, he's starred in stage musical Godspell, as well as touring with History of Big Bands and Boogie Nights.

    7. RHYDIAN ROBERTS

    ©  Christie Goodwin/Getty Images

    Controversial contestant Rhydian Roberts divided the nation with his theatrics on the show, but ultimately finished behind winner Leon Jackson back in 2007. Simon Cowell was a fan of his eccentric showmanship, and helped him release his eponymous debut album in 2008. It went on to sell over 600,000 copies, which is nothing to be sniffed at at all.

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    Second album O Fortuna followed a year later, but that unfortunately stalled at No.25. Cowell soon cut ties, but Rhydian went on to release three more albums nonetheless. The most recent, 2014's One Day Like This, was funded by fans on PledgeMusic and allowed the Welsh singer to go independent. It went on to spend 10 consecutive weeks atop the Official Classical Album chart. Not bad at all.

    6. RAY QUINN

    ©  PA Images Ian West / PA Archive

    Former Brookside actor Ray Quinn was a gracious runner-up back in 2006, losing out to The X Factor's most successful winner so far, Leona Lewis. But Ray still had his time to shine, and put out his own big band collection, Doing It My Way, the following year.

    That second album is still in limbo, but is apparently on the way. Ray has, however, kept himself firmly in the spotlight with a number of high profile talent show appearance and a few major roles in stage shows up and down the country.

    5. FLEUR EAST

    ©  Syco

    Fleur East may have forced Mark Ronson and Bruno Mars to rush release 'Uptown Funk' after her own version from the show scaled iTunes, but it didn't secure her enough votes in the final to fend off eventual winner Ben Haenow. What it did do, though, was give her the blueprint for her own fantastic debut single 'Sax'. It all but dominated radio and television (via an Asda Christmas advert) upon its release, peaking at number three in the UK.

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    Accompanying album Love, Sax and Flashbacks didn't fare as well, stumbling at No.14, but Fleur is already hard at work on its follow-up. The 28-year-old has also enjoyed some international success with 'Sax' across Europe and Australia, as well as giving a few high profile performances on American television this past spring.

    4. G4

    Classical quintet G4 may have lost out to X Factor's first ever winner Steve Brookstein back in 2004, but that didn't stop them becoming one of the more successful runners-up in the show's history. Shortly after the final they enjoyed a chart-topping album, while its follow-up (a mere 9 months later) was also certified Platinum.

    That being said, the group split in 2007, and band member Jonathan Ansell went on to top the classical chart with his solo debut Tenor at the Movies. More recently they reunited, and a fourth album, G4 At Christmas, was released in November 2015. Just this year they embarked on their Back 4 Good UK tour, which will included 15 dates across the country. All these years later and they're still doing OK.

    3. REBECCA FERGUSON

    Rebecca Ferguson narrowly lost out to Matt Cardle in 2010 (what is it with contestants from Liverpool always finishing second?), but the singer-songwriter had the last laugh, wowing critics with her stunning debut album Heaven. It may have missed out on reaching the UK top spot, but it went on to sell an impressive 1.5 million copies worldwide.

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    2013 follow-up Freedom was also certified Gold in the UK, while just last year Rebecca covered the whole of Billie Holiday's Lady Sings the Blues to mark the legendary singer's 100th birthday. But there's more to come yet: the star recently confirmed that she has finished her fourth studio album and we could be getting new music very soon indeed.

    2. JLS

    ©  venni

    In some ways, JLS's defeat on the X Factor final back in 2008 was Beyoncé's fault. Eventual winner Alexandra Burke's duet with the US superstar was television gold, while JLS were lumbered with Westlife. That being said, the four-piece boyband were soon the new princes of pop, and their debut single 'Beat Again' was a deserved chart-topper that earned them two Brit Awards.

    Across their post-X Factor career, JLS scored five No.1 singles and four Top 3 albums, and sold out arenas across the country. They even travelled to Australia and the US, generating a bit of buzz for themselves internationally. However, as soon as One Direction came along and monopolised the boyband market, JLS decided to call it a day. After a huge 10 million records sold in five years, the British boyband had cemented their place as one of the most successful X Factor runners-up ever.

    1. OLLY MURS

    ©  Yu Tsai

    We're not going to lie: when Olly Murs finished behind Joe McElderry back in 2009, we thought the most he'd achieve would be a covers album of soul classics ready for Mother's Day and then maybe a bit of West End theatre work. But the cheeky chap from Essex nailed his debut single 'Please Don't Let Me Go', scoring a UK chart-topper right out of the box. Three more No.1 singles have followed since, as well as three chart-topping albums (and his No.2 debut).

    Although Olly hasn't properly cracked America, he's certainly made his presence felt across Europe and Australia with singles like 'Heart Skips a Beat', 'Troublemaker' and 'Dear Darlin''. His total sales so far easily top 10 million, and that's before he unleashes his highly-anticipated fifth studio album later this year. Even outside of music, Olly has been a hit - his stint as Xtra Factor host went so well that he got promoted to the main show last year, giving contestants a much-needed hug.

    It's nothing short of remarkable what Olly Murs has achieved as an X Factor runner-up - especially when everyone else from his year has all but slipped out of the spotlight.
