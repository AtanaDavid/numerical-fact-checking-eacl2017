Results No Longer Masking City's Plight

What's being said in the forum now?
Click to join the Vital Coventry forum!

This time last season, if we ever dared to assume anything about Coventry City`s future it was that a deal would probably be done with Wasps that would see the Sky Blues remaining at the Ricoh Arena for the long-term.

The subject was a little on the back burner, partly because it was relatively quiet on the off-field front, as Tony Mowbray`s team were scoring goals, winning games and entertaining their supporters.

Good results can go some, not all, of the way to masking unease about the direction in which a club is heading but the upcoming winter months look like being difficult ones on both fronts.

On the pitch, City`s squad does seem slightly weaker than this time last year and without the type of pace and positivity up front that genuinely had opposition defences fearful of facing the Sky Blues.

That is something that will all come out in the wash over the next few months, part of the usual ups and downs of a season and, who knows, it may be that there is an enjoyable end to this campaign after a difficult start.

But it is clear that the off-field issues not only remain but are getting steadily worse as time goes on.

What's happening with the court cases? The Academy? The news stadium? The Ricoh? All the key questions answered here https://t.co/mq4dcDw8iT

— Coventry Telegraph (@CovTel_CCFC) September 9, 2016



The Coventry Telegraph continues to do its best but the inevitable end result seems to be drawing ever nearer with each piece of bad news, whether it be on the stadium or the academy.
