Puskás Award

© FIFA.com

Goals are the ultimate purpose of the game of football, and many professional players and coaches - as well as most supporters – can describe their favourite strike vividly many years later. Indeed, a discussion amongst fans around the world of football takes place almost every day as to the best goal they have seen that week – or in their lifetime. That discussion was opened up to a public vote in 2009, when the shortlist of ten nominees for the annual FIFA Puskás Award was announced.

It was perhaps fitting that Cristiano Ronaldo, who had signed for Real Madrid in the summer of 2009, took home the inaugural Puskás Award – honouring the late Hungarian legend who scored countless stunning goals for Real Madrid (even if the award-winning goal was scored for Manchester United).

The criteria are fairly simple: an aesthetically beautiful goal, awarded without distinction of championship, gender or nationality, scored without the result of luck or a mistake and in support of Fair Play.  

Who will join Cristiano Ronaldo, Neymar and Zlatan Ibrahimovic on the list of winners and take home the coveted prize this year? A list of ten candidates for the The FIFA Puskás Award, drawn up by an experts' panel integrated by Marco van Basten, Marta and Gabriel Batisuta, will be announced in November. From that final shortlist, a public vote will ensue, with the three winners from that first voting stage becoming the final nominees, where a second voting stage will open. These three will be announced at a later stage.

The winners of all of the awards will be revealed at the The Best FIFA Football Awards show in Zurich on 9 January.
