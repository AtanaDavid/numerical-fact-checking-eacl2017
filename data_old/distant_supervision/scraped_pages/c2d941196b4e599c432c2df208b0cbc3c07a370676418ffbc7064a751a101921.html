EU leaders press on with migration ‘containment’ plans
June 29, 2016

 

 

 

 

EU leaders agreed immigration containment plans at their meeting in Brussels on 28 June.  Despite strong pressure from over 100 NGOs and campaign groups to adopt a more humane stance, the EU bosses agreed to:

 

  • fast and operational returns of irregular migrants
  • use development and trade to put a brake on migration from outside EU borders;
  • to collaborate on stemming migration from ‘specific countries’.

The meeting concluded that:

“Cooperation on readmission and return will be a key test of the partnership between the EU and these partners”.

Local leaders must condemn racist attacks - Amnesty
June 29, 2016

 

 

Human rights body, Amnesty International is calling on local leaders to condemn the spate of racist attacks and incidents in the wake of the 23 June pro—Brexit vote.

 

In an on line statement, Amnesty says:

“Violence. Vandalism. Hate speech. Racist slogans on t-shirts. In the last few days, reports of hate crimes in Britain have increased. Fuelled by years of hostile rhetoric, coupled with divisive campaigns, we are now seeing racism and xenophobia on the rise on our streets and in our communities.

“Local leaders must condemn these actions immediately and do everything in their power to make people safe and welcome. Tell your council to speak out against racism, xenophobia and hate crimes now.”

NGOs slam new EU migration containment plans
June 26, 2016

Over 100 NGOs, including MRN, have called on European leaders meeting at the European Council this week to develop a sustainable long-term strategy for migration management, in consultation with civil society and experts.

They have also called for:

  • safe and regular channels to Europe both for those in need of international protection and other migrants
  • a rejection of plans to use international aid money to pay developing countries to control migration on the EU's behalf
  • a halt to sending people back to third countries that violate – or risk violating – fundamental rights and the rule of law
  • transparency in managing migration and accountability for human rights violations arising from EU policies
  • foreign policy and action aimed at preventing and unlocking protracted crises

Sort out EU nationals’ status before you go Cameron – New Europeans
June 26, 2016

 

Euro-campaigning body New Europeans has handed a letter in to Downing Street calling on outgoing PM, David Cameron, to sort out the long-term status of EU nationals living and working in the UK before he leaves office.

The letter urges Cameron to go beyond his statement that “nothing is going to change immediately”, and goes on to point out that EU citizens living in the UK and British citizens living in other EU member states need to know now that their long-term position and that of their families is secure.

It goes on to call on the PM to secure safeguards and commitments from EU member states, and from all UK parliamentary parties as soon as possible.

Petition for EU citizens to stay
June 24, 2016

 

 

A petition has been launched demanding an unequivocal statement on the status of EU citizens living and working in the UK. Think-tank British Future says that a clear commitment is needed - from the government, politicians of all parties and from the British people – that this is their home and they continue to be welcome here.

It calls on the government to make clear that its policy will be to protect the rights of EU citizens living and working in the UK and to seek reciprocal arrangements for UK citizens in other EU countries.
