Southampton 4-2 Manchester City Barclays Premier League RESULT: Sadio Mane grabs hat-trick as Manuel Pellegrini's men struggle

  • Shane Long gave Southampton a deserved lead with an instinctive finish
  • Sadio Mane doubled his side's lead three minutes later with a fine strike

Sadio Mane scored a hat-trick as Southampton moved above Liverpool into seventh place in the Barclays Premier League courtesy of a thumping 4-2 win over a much-changed but woeful Manchester City at St Mary's.

Shane Long broke the deadlock with his 100th goal in English football with finish from eight yards after 25 minutes, and the Saints doubled their lead just three minutes later as Dusan Tadic provided his second assist with Mane the beneficiary after surging into the box to clip the ball over Joe Hart.

City were handed a lifeline before the break as Kelechi Iheanacho scored his eighth goal in nine starts this season with a close-range header after Samir Nasri's cross had rebounded off Cuco Martina.

Southampton restored their two-goal lead as Mane scrambled in his second after Hart had tipped Virgil Van Dijk's header onto the crossbar, and the Senegal international completed his treble 11 minutes later with another clinical finish at the near post, before the impressive Iheanacho curled in his second of the afternoon.

PRE-MATCH STATS: Southampton vs Manchester City

Southampton have won just one of their last nine Premier League games against Manchester City, with six defeats and two draws in that time.

Raheem Sterling has had a hand in five goals in his last four Premier League matches against the Saints (three goals, two assists).

Joe Hart could make his 300th Premier League appearance in this match and would be the sixth English goalkeeper to reach this landmark in the competition.

Front-man Sergio Aguero has scored in each of his last five league games, but has never scored in six in a row in the competition.

Ronald Koeman's side have fired in the most open play crosses in the Premier League this season (654) and have scored the most headed goals (14).

Virgil van Dijk has won 74 per cent of his 206 aerial duels in the league this season; the highest ratio of any player to have been involved in 100+ aerials.

This is now Shane Long's best goalscoring season in the Premier League (nine goals) and the Irish striker has had a hand in eight goals in his last eight league appearances (four goals, four assists).

Sadio Mane has scored four goals in his last five Premier League games for Southampton; after a run of 19 league appearances without a goal.

City have conceded a goal every 78 minutes without Vincent Kompany on the pitch this season, compared to a goal conceded every 180 minutes when he has been on the pitch (including injury time).

Kevin De Bruyne has created the most goalscoring chances for Man City this season in the Premier League (71) despite appearing in only 23 of their 35 games.

Real Madrid clash on Pellegrini's mind

ADAM SHERGOLD AT ST MARY'S: Little surprise to see Manuel Pellegrini shuffle his pack here at St Mary’s, with five changes from the team that beat Stoke 4-0 last weekend and eight from the team that drew 0-0 with Real Madrid in the first leg of the Champions League semi-final in the week.

With one eye on that trip to the Bernabeu on Wednesday night, Pellegrini benches Bacary Sagna, Fernando, Sergio Aguero, Jesus Navas and Gael Clichy. Among those coming in are Wilfried Bony, Raheem Sterling, Eliaquim Mangala, Fabian Delph and Kelechi Iheanacho.

City enter the game four points ahead of Manchester United and their fourth spot and Champions League football will certainly look secure if they extend that to seven.

The fans in town here ahead of the game have a Champions League win on their minds though, singing a song about going to the San Siro for the final on May 28.

Southampton make three changes from the team that won 4-2 at Aston Villa, with Cuco Martina, Jordy Clasie and Sadio Mane coming into the team.
