Garcia and Cabrera-Bello hope to emulate Spanish heroics of Ballesteros and Olazabal

IT HAS been 23 years since the Spanish Armada of Seve Ballesteros and Jose Maria Olazabal last sailed together in the Ryder Cup but Europe’s hope is that they have assembled another destructive Iberian duo for Hazeltine.

By Neil Squires
PUBLISHED: PUBLISHED: 22:30, Thu, Sep 29, 2016

Ballesteros and Olazabal are an impossible act to follow with their astonishing record of 13 points out of 15 together but Sergio Garcia and Rafa Cabrera-Bello share a burning desire to fire some cannonballs across America’s bows. 

It will be a blend of the old and the new as far as the Ryder Cup is concerned with Garcia, a football-loving European grandee taking part in his eighth instalment, and Cabrera-Bello, a surf and ski dude playing in his first. 

“I have dreamed of playing Ryder Cup since I was a teenager. I’ve watched tons of videos and heard stories from Miguel (Angel Jimenez), Sergio and Olly. It has always been a long-term goal,” said Cabrera-Bello.

    “I think I’m the tenth Spanish player to participate in the Ryder Cup so that is a huge honour for me. It has a huge importance in Spanish golf. Everyone in Spain knows what Ryder Cup is and for all us golfers it has always been something that we have been fighting towards. “I’d love to play with Sergio. 

    He has done huge things over the past 15 years for Ryder Cup and Spanish golf. He has been the best Spanish player in that time, no question. If I have the honour to play with him then great. “If we dare to compare ourselves to Seve and Olly, I think that’s a very bold comparison to make. Maybe if we are repeating this conversation in three or four Ryder Cups then we could consider it. That is way down the line.”

    When it comes to the Ryder Cup, the Sunday singles appear decisive but in reality it is the team play that has the bigger effect on the outcome.

    Sixteen of the 28 points on offer come in the fourballs – where each player uses his own ball and the best score on each hole counts – and the foursomes – where the teammates share one ball. 

    Pairing the right men together is part-art, part-science. It can revolve around matching temperament, distance or, in foursomes, players who use same the same type of ball. Comfort in each other’s company is also a big part of the equation – Olazabal and Ballesteros, like Europe’s second most potent partnership Darren Clarke and Lee Westwood, were great friends. Garcia’s link with Cabrera-Bello stretches back 24 years to the Spanish national junior championships. 

    “I first met his mother when I was eight years old. She happened to be my scorer in a Spanish under-10 championship in Madrid. Sergio was playing in the under 12s and winning it,” said Cabrera-Bello. 

    “For me Sergio is the most talented player in the world so playing with him will be the easiest thing ever knowing he can hit any single shot there is in golf. I’m a great family friend of his. We’ve known each other for a long time. Hopefully we can play together and play really well.” 

    Garcia will undoubtedly be the lead man – the Ballesteros role – but third place in this year’s World Matchplay in Texas underlined his junior partner’s right to share golf’s most exciting stage. 

    “I think the fact that I have played well in America is going to give me extra confidence. I’ve played against all the players we are facing and I’m sure I’ve beaten them all in some tournament or another this year. It is good to know I can play well on American soil,” said Cabrera-Bello. “I’ve asked Sergio and other players about the Ryder Cup. They say the emotions and the excitement you feel in a Ryder Cup are hard to compare to anything else so I’m really looking forward to it.”
