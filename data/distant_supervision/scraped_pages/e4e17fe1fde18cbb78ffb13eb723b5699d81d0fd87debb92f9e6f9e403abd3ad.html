Chicago Monthly Climate Averages

Spring

Spring arrives for a very short period in Chicago between April and May. Whilst fog is most frequent during this season, the uppermost regular day temperature is 15°C with the lowest average temperature 5°C.

Thunderstorms are prevalent during spring time in Chicago, with the season a largely unpredictable one. The highest temperature recorded in this season is 33°C in May, with -22°C the lowest temperature recorded in preceding month of March.

Summer

Spanning across June-August, summers in Chicago usually feature moderately high temperatures that regularly climb to 32°C.

Summer is both the rainiest and sunniest season in Chicago, with the season prone to thunderstorms but also a vast amount of possible sunshine.

The average maximum temperature reaches 28°C in Chicago, with the highest temperature recorded as 41C in July and the lowest average temperature standing at 16°C.

Autumn

The extreme heat from the summer in Chicago can offer persist into the autumn season, with weather relatively unpredictable during the September-November months.

Whilst still hot, Chicago can be chilly, rainy or even a tad moist during autumn. September can produce the highest temperature of up to 36°C, whilst November will usually experience a lower temperature of 13°C.

Winter

The winter is often in vast comparison to the summers of Chicago, with snowfall a regular feature that provides an average of 38 inches of snow on average.

Snow will often cover the Chicago land for a few weeks, but temperatures on the whole can be quite variable. The highest average temperature is 10°C, with the lowest average temperature around -9°C.

Extremes have been known to hit Chicago during this season, with visitors in January potentially experiencing a rather intolerable -29°C and contrastingly, 22°C in February.
