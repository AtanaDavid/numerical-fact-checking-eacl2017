KEYNOTE SPEAKER

Humza Yousaf MSP

Minister for Transport and the Islands, Scottish Government

Humza Yousaf was born in Glasgow on 7 April 1985. He was educated at Hutchesons’ Grammar School and the University of Glasgow, graduating with a degree in Politics. He is also an alumnus of the US State Department's prestigious International Visitor Learning Programme.

He went on to work in the Scottish Parliament as an aide to the late Bashir Ahmad MSP before he was elected as an MSP for the Glasgow Region in May 2011. He was a Parliamentary Liaison Officer to former First Minister Alex Salmond MSP and was appointed Minister for External Affairs and International Development in September 2012.  He was appointed Minister for Transport and the Islands in May 2016.

HumzaYousaf

Kirsty MacArthur

Legal Director, MacArthur Green

Kirsty is Director of award winning environmental consultancy, MacArthur Green. As a qualified solicitor, Kirsty is responsible for the management of the legal issues and operations for the company. Prior to MacArthur Green, Kirsty worked as an Associate for Pinsent Masons LLP, and legal advisor for ScottishPower Renewables.

MacArthur Green has the pleasure of being environmental advisors to CalMac, and count among our projects the bird, ecology and hydrology surveys for CH2M/Fairhursts on Lot 1 of the A9 Dualling project, and Atkins/Mouchel for Lot 3, where we are approved consultants for Transport Scotland).

 MacArthurGreen

Charlotte Twyning

Executive Director - Policy, Strategy & Communications, Abellio UK

Charlotte is Director of Policy, Strategy and Communications for Abellio UK which currently operates the ScotRail and Greater Anglia rail franchises, along with the Merseyrail concession. In this role, she leads on the implementation of the UK business strategy and works with colleagues to identify opportunities to grow the company’s market share.

With her team, Charlotte is responsible for developing policy positions that help deliver this strategy, and for effectively communicating it to the government, the media, and the wider rail industry, ensuring Abellio has a strong voice in the debates that matter.

Having originally trained as a lawyer,Charlotte most recently worked at BT in several senior positions, including Programme Director for Customer Service Strategy, as well as in Operational Regulation.

Jonathan Pugh

Head of Strategy & Planning Scotland, Network Rail

Jonathan joined Network Rail in February 2016.  A transport professional for nearly three decades, he joined British Rail’s management training scheme in 1989, and has worked in strategic and commercial roles across the industry and government.  He moved from the former Strategic Rail Authority to Transport Scotland in 2005, where he worked as Head of Planning and Regulation until 2010.  He moved to ATOC/Rail Delivery Group to work on strategic planning and franchising issues, and was a member of Sir Roy McNulty’s Rail Value For Money study in 2010-11.  An economist by training, he is a Chartered Fellow of the Institute of Logistics and Transport.
networkrail

Mustaq Pangarkar

Director (Rail). Patrick Parsons

Mustaq has over 27 years experience in rail, structural and civil engineering projects, from project inception through to site construction and project completion and has been responsible for the management and co-ordination of design teams and programme and financial delivery of numerous projects.

He has been responsible for liaison with the relevant sections of Network Rail, Local Planning Authority, Local Highways Authority and other parties either directly or via the project manager, the management and co-ordination of design teams and programme and financial delivery of numerous projects. The previous project categories include design and construction Grade level car parks, design and refurbishment of multi-storey car parks, project management of multi-discipline projects, contract management under JCT, ICE and ECC contracts, local Authority and Housing Association schemes, institutional buildings such as Fire Stations, Police Headquarters, primary and secondary Schools, Healthcare Agricultural and Industrial Units and retail units and shopping centres and providing information as necessary to secure Network Rail approvals via Forms F001, F002 and F003.

David Hytch

Technology & Smart Ticketing & Reservations Lead, CalMac Ferries

David has been active in transport technology and innovation for 22 years. He is currently the Technology lead for the introduction of Smart Ticketing and Reservations for Calmac Ferries including passengers and vehicles, and multi modal products with other Scottish transport modes.

He successfully directed the CIO, Highways Management, Road Safety and Environment management functions for Transport for Greater Manchester.

David has led Smart Ticketing activities for Rail, Bus, Tram and Ferries including ITSO and non ITSO schemes and including Passengers, Freight and Vehicle ticketing and reservations. David serves on the the European Program Committee for the ITS World Congress and as an assessor for Innovation UK on Intelligent mobility and Autonomous Vehicles.

He has served on the Automotive Council Technology group, DfT expert panel for Strategic Road development, and Accessible Transport bodies. 

David is a Director of Parkgate Consultants working on a wide range of projects and assignments in the Transport and Technology arena, including Franchise Bids, Supplier assessments and Programme delivery.

CalMacFerries

Neil Amner

Consultant, Curle Stewart

From a background in commercial property work, Neil has developed a strong track record as one of Scotland’s leading transport, environmental and parliamentary lawyers.

Neil has worked on rail sector matters since privatisation. Chambers & Partners’ Guide to the Legal Profession in Scotland has again listed him as a leading individual for Transport (Rail).

He is a Chartered Fellow of the Chartered Institute of Logistics & Transport UK and acts as General Counsel to the European Freight & Logistics Leaders Forum.

His transport practise encompasses contractual and regulatory aspects of rail (passenger and freight), tram, roads, logistics, bus / coach, aviation, ferry and short sea shipping and harbours operational and infrastructure matters.

Neil is a member of the Law Society of Scotland's Environmental Law Committee, the United Kingdom Environmental Law Association and a Director of the Glasgow Building Preservation Trust.

He is known for pragmatic commercial as well as detailed legal advice.  Neil became the President of Glasgow Chamber of Commerce in January 2016 and is a member of the Board of Scottish Chambers of Commerce and chairs its Economic Advisory Group.

Examples of professional work

  • Lead the external legal team advising Transport Scotland on the procurement of the new ScotRail and Caledonian Sleeper franchise contracts, the first passenger rail franchises to be awarded by the Scottish Ministers. The ScotRail franchise has a contract value of over £10 billion.
  • Lead the external legal team on negotiation for Siemens plc of the Infraco contract for the Edinburgh tram network.

  • Acted for Port Babcock Rosyth Limited in drafting and securing grant of the harbour orders which respectively  made Babcock the statutory harbour authority for the Rosyth dockyard and authorises the construction of Rosyth International Container Terminal. Neil also drafted the Construction and Environmental Management Plan which was key to the second order following a public inquiry.

  • Lead the external legal team for Clackmannanshire Council (as authorised undertaker) on the delivery of the Stirling-Alloa-Kincardine railway and the associated Alloa relief road project, including negotiating risk allocation and handover agreements with Network Rail.

  • Acted for ScottishPower in the Judicial Review in which Neil’s approach to rationalising case law on end of “waste” status was adopted by Lord Reed in the Court of Session and subsequently by the English Court of Appeal in another case.

  • Acted for SEPA in the appeals against two refusals of PPC Permit applications. Neil secured the regulatory outcome sought by SEPA without the expense and operational impact, or residual regulatory liability, of the appeals being heard.

  • In represented a variety of clients on a range of Bills before the Scottish Parliament, secured amendments to the Bills and / or undertakings to protect the value of land adjacent to infrastructure projects or otherwise to protect business interests.

    CurleStewart

Duncan Sutherland

Board Member of HS2 and Group Regeneration Director of Sigma Capital Group

Duncan Sutherland is the Group Regeneration Director for Sigma Capital PLC and a Non-Executive Director on the board of High Speed2 (HS2).

Duncan works closely with the government promoting innovative partnership approaches to achieving regeneration and investment. Previously, Duncan was Director of City Development for Coventry City Council; worked at Inner City Enterprises PLC, responsible for major regeneration schemes in Manchester, Liverpool, and Nottingham; and was also the Chief Executive of The EDI Group Ltd, a specialist investment/property development company.

On HS2, Duncan brings regeneration and property expertise to the Board. He was a Non Executive Director on the board of British Waterways and Scottish Canals for eight years, is a member of the Regeneration Investment Organisation Advisory Board (part of UKTI) and a Trustee of the South Bank Sinfonia.

SigmaCapitalPLC 

Tim Hedley-Jones

Major Projects Director, Virgin Trains East Coast

Leading the East Coast Property and Projects team to deliver station enhancements at our 12 stations and East Coast's involvement in the Intercity Express Project to provide new trains for the franchise. The team also includes Procurement and Compliance (including the management of East Coast relationships with the DfT).

Key projects delivered by the Property Team include a major £8.1m new station building at Wakefield Westgate, a £3m refurbishment of Peterborough Station, the introduction of a new accessible Travel Centre counter across the route and new pedestrian/cycle entrances at York Station. The team also manages commercial retail revenue at stations and has delivered major developments at York, Wakefield and Doncaster. Current projects include the redevelopment of Newcastle Central station and new platform waiting rooms at York station.

Joined Intercity East Coast franchise in 1999 and have worked for GNER, NXEC and now ECML Co Ltd in a variety of management positions and, since 2007, as a Director.

Specialties:

  • UK rail industry regulatory environment
  • Franchising including franchise bidding
  • Leading change programmes
  • Station Management and Enhancement

Initial 8 years of Time's career were spent with HM Diplomatic Service including working on an Eastern European Technical Aid programme, UN Sanctions and a secondment to the Department for Trade & Industry. Also a 3-year posting to the British Embassy Moscow.

Subsequently took a year out to complete an MA in Archaeological Heritage Management.
