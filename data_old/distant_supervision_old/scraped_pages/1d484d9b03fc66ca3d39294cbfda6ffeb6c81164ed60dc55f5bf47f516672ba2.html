#191 Dustin Wimmer Dominates 33rd Motorama Arenacross by sweeping all four Open A & Pro-Am Class Main events over the Weekend
Harrisburg, PA - The 33rd Annual Motorama ATV Arenacross racing weekend was dominated by multi-time AMA Pro ATV MX champion, 191 Dustin Wimmer, as he claimed all four main event wins on Saturday and Sunday in the Open A and Pro-Am classes.

Motorama is not just a single event, but offers something for the entire family all under one roof, which attracts over 40,000 spectators and over 2,000 racers annually. The farm show complex host a wide array of events and contest from the arenacross, radio control racing, robot conflict, car show, Go-Kart Racing, Quarter Midget Racing, Hot Rod & Speed Car Show, Tunerwar, Spina-Bifida Charity Auction, and Ms. Motorama Pageant.
For 2011, the Motorama Arenacross track was run in reverse

As always, the Farm Show Arena was packed to capacity with over 8,000 spectators and many standing in the stairwells, trying to get a glimpse of the ATV and motorcycle racing action with 27 Main events on both Saturday and Sunday.
#191 Dustin Wimmer & Mechanic, Glenn Schlude
Dustin Wimmer made a surprise appearance at the 2011 Motorama, which was his first attendance since 2004. For the past six years, Wimmer has focused his attention on WPSA and AMA ATV Motocross Nationals, but with Suzuki pulling out of the Nationals, the two-time Pro ATV champion, was left without a ride for the nationals, so for 2011, Wimmer will focus his attention on the closer to home series, New England ATV Motocross Series, which kicks off at Walden MX in New York on April 30th.

Wimmer may not have a full blown factory ride for 2011, be he proved that he hasn’t lost any of this speed as he dominated the Arenacross racing by claiming all four main event wins over the weekend.
Dustin Wimmer got the jump on the field for the Holeshot in the Pro-Am Race

On Saturday, Wimmer grabbed the Holeshot in the Open A class and checked out on the competition as he lead from start to finish. In the Pro-Am race, Wimmer once again got the jump off the line and went on to claim yet another win.
