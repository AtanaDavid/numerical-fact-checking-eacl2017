Ranger: runner-up in 2016 NPS Championship

Andrew Ranger (No. 27 Mopar/Pennzoil/Mopar Express Lane Dodge) finished 14th Sunday's Pinty's Series (NPS) season-ending Kawartha 250 at Kawartha Speedway in Fraserville, Ontario.

He started eighth in the lineup and completed 203 laps of the 250-lap event. Ranger, who started in all 12 NPS races this season, ended up second in the final championship standings, 54 points behind 2016 NPS Champion Cayden Lapcevich.

Austin Dillon crashed out of AAA 500

Pole sitter Austin Dillon crashed on lap 262 of 293 and finished 37th.

Dillon spent 55.3 percent of the race among the top five and 98.9 percent of the laps inside the top 10. He had a legitimate shot at finishing in the top five and was wrecked in an incident with Kevin Harvick while battling for fifth. This team does not have enough opportunities like that to make them a place-and-hold fantasy value, but more importantly the frustration associated with getting wrecked in that circumstance could have a ripple effect for Harvick.

Another bad finish puts Ku Busch in hole

Kurt Busch finished one lap off the pace in 20th in the AAA Texas 500 at Texas Motor Speedway.

That is the second consecutive race in which Busch finished in the 20s. It puts him at a 34-point deficit entering a race where nearly every competitor is stronger than him. He cannot make up enough ground to advance to the Chase finale without gambling and that will make him a difficult fantasy choice.

Jimmie Johnson gambles, finishes 11th

Jimmie Johnson finished 11th in the AAA Texas 500 after he pitted for tires during the final caution for rain.

Johnson was running sixth when the rains fell, but he gambled that NASCAR would dry the track and get back to racing. They didn’t and he lost five spots and critical points for fantasy owners, but he had nothing to lose since he is already locked into the Chase finale. That is one of the risks associated with a driver in his position.

7th-place Kenseth one point behind bubble

Matt Kenseth finished seventh in the AAA Texas 500 at Texas Motor Speedway, which puts him one point behind the bubble to make the Chase finale in Homestead-Miami Speedway.

This was the first top-10 for Kenseth at Texas since he finished seventh in the spring 2014 race. Joe Gibbs Racing is solid on short, flat tracks but so are Penske Racing and Kevin Harvick so next week is going to hotly contested. Expect six or seven Chase contenders to finish in the top 10 next week.

Harvick 6th at Texas, but creates enemy

Kevin Harvick finished sixth in the AAA 500 at Texas Motor Speedway, but after contact with Austin Dillon in the late stages of the race it could be a pyrrhic top-10.

Harvick raced Dillon hard near the end of the AAA 500 and took the air off his spoiler. Dillon wiggled in front of the No. 4, was contacted, and hit the wall. On the way to the garage, his crew chief mentioned how much more Harvick had to lose at Phoenix. While it is unlikely that Dillon will purposefully wreck the 4, he will certainly race him hard if he has the opportunity. Harvick is 18 points behind the cutoff and practically in a must-win situation.

Chase Elliott finished fourth in the AAA Texas 500 at Texas Motor Speedway.

Elliott finished fifth in his Texas debut this spring, so he improved by one spot. The No. 24 has either been great or suffered catastrophe on the similarly-configured, 1.5-mile tracks in 2016. He will be a favorite to finish in the top five at Homestead-Miami Speedway in two weeks.

Martin Truex Jr., 3rd at Texas

Martin Truex Jr. finished third at Texas Motor Speedway after starting 12th in the AAA 500.

Truex has not been perfect at Texas during his career, but this is now his fourth consecutive top-10. It is his first top-five on this track since spring 2013 and he is making a statement about how he would have performed if not for a blown engine at Talladega SuperSpeedway. He is not only fantasy relevant, he is a must-have in most games.
