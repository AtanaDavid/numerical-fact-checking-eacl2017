I found that so far, this course/tourney required the most patience and learning from prior rounds.  After the first round, I learned a lot, which helped me keep my scores lower (at least compared to others):
1) Avoid the rough like the plague.  You can not hit out of it.  And if you do, your aim will be off.
2) Do not tempt water.  Go long if you have to.  Almost everyone who had a bad round started with hitting the water on the 2nd hole.  I always went long and relied on my pitching to keep par.
3) A couple of holes had very slopey fairways.  Once learned, stay to the opposite side and let the ball role, otherwise you found rough.
4) Play for par on every hole and every once in a while, you will have a birdie opportunity.  This is where the patience came in.  Use shorter clubs off the tee to stay away from rough and use shorter to the green because it was fast.
5) You need good pitching/lobbing especially out of traps (which I found quite often) and out of bad lies (tons around the green).  If you can't recover with a pitch or lob, you will score high since nothing stuck to the greens.
6) Do not try for a low score, instead rely on par and the other players messing up because you can not get a birdie when you want.  They were few and far between.  But those bogeys just kept coming.  Though it looked like it was a blowout, Nicklaus actually only had a one stroke lead going into the 13th hole.  The 14th was a par 5 where you could birdie, but it was not easy.  Nicklaus parred 13, 14, 15, 16 and watched as everyone else bogeyed multiple holes to extend his lead.  So by the time he got to 17 and bogeyed, he was safe.
