    18 1/2 is an accurate placement of the O/U for the number of teams next season. Tough call! I will take the Over, but the final number of teams will be closer to 19 than the mid-20s. Given the relaxed standards for joining the MASL (look up Dion Earl or Thomas Gurriero for more info on that), I expect several more expansion teams. These teams will mostly be smaller arena but hopefully at least one viable larger arena team. The decent attendance gain for some teams, low cost structure (for small arena teams) & the novelty of a new 'national' league will attract expansion interest.

    A couple of guys in Toledo have talked about joining the MASL ( though they don't seem to have much $$), and Milliken claims to have a lot of interest from potential teams (comments taken with a large grain of salt). Milwaukee had a large attendance drop, so I wonder about their viability, though I think they will likely be back-somehow they survived several changes in ownership. Tulsa is likely gone, unless they find another owner/fool willing to lose a lot of $$. In the spirit of the 'lose&gain teams' routine of the recent MISL incarnation, the MASL loses 5, gains the new Mexican team, plus gains a couple more to end up at 20.

    Given the low bar of admission to the MASL, Youngstown is in as long as they can afford road game travel & their franchise fee check clears the bank. I agree that medium sized markets with 3,000+ capacity arenas should be a focus of expansion efforts. There are not as many other concerts & sports teams to compete with, so it should be easier to sell sufficient seats & sponsorships to break even. Many large city arena rents are way too expensive for sports like indoor soccer.

    While we recognize the minor status of indoor soccer, I also think that the teams in rec centers should either move to arenas with at least 2,000 capacity or be relegated to the amateur ranks. I would be less concerned with giving the Sockers and Dallas a few extra teams to beat up & more concerned about my league's teams being in a real arena and having professional standards. Then the league could establish minimum player compensation and benefits, mandated worker comp coverage, consistent field size/quality, etc. I know that these things are unrealistic for the MASL, but I can dream!
