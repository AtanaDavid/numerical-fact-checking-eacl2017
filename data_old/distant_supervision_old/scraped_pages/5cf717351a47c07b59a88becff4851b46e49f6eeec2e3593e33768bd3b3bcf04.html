FLASHBACK: In 1964, a crowd of 10,000 watches Catasauqua win District 11 title

March 02, 2014|From Call staff reports

2009—Allen grad Darnell Braswell scores 27 points and grabs 13 rebounds to lead DeSales to a 77-71 win at Ithaca, giving the Bulldogs their first-ever NCAA Division III men's basketball Sweet 16 berth.

2004—Jose Olivero and Kevin Tempest each score 17 points and Lehigh beats Bucknell 60-45 in Upper Marlboro, Md., to reach the Patriot League men's basketball finals. The top-seeded Mountain Hawks will host second-seed American at Stabler Arena in search of their first Patriot League title and first NCAA tournament berth since 1988.

2004—Lehigh crowns three champs (Troy Letters, Brad Dillong and Travis Frick), has four wrestlers place second and qualifies eight for the NCAA tournament while winning its third straight Eastern Intercollegiate Wrestling Association title, the first time the Mountain Hawks have won three straight since 1975-77.

1969—Tony DaRe's fifth steal of the game with six seconds left clinches Central Catholic's 61-60 win over previously unbeaten Scranton Prep and puts the Vikings into the Pennsylvania Catholic Interscholastic Athletic Association's East Regional championship game. DaRe scores 18 points while John Caspar scores 14 points and pulls down 19 rebounds for Central (17-6), which avenges a 1968 playoff loss to Prep (18-1).

1964—A crowd of over 10,000 at the Harrisburg Farm Show Arena, including a record paid attendance of 9,649, watches Catasauqua (23-1) become the fourth team to win three straight District 11 titles by beating Nazareth 70-59 in the Class A final. Larry Miller scores 38 points and grabs 25 rebounds while Walt Panik adds 17 points for the Rough Riders, who win their fourth title in five years. The loss snaps an 18-game win streak for Nazareth (20-3). In the first game of the doubleheader, Fountain Hill (16-7) surprises Pine Grove (23-2) by a 48-43 score for its third straight Class B title and sixth in 10 years.

March 8

2009—Lafayette (10-21), which stunned second-seeded American 58-56 in the opening round, reaches the finals of the Patriot League's postseason women's basketball tournament by beating Navy 62-55. The Leopards, who hadn't won a league tournament game since 2000 and who had come into the tournament on a 10-game losing streak, will travel to top-seeded Lehigh (25-6), a 61-42 winner over Army, for the championship and an NCAA Tournament berth.

1974—John McLaughlin's twisting layup with three seconds left, his only field goal of the night, lifts Panther Valley to a 22-21 District 11 Class B boys basketball championship game win over Blue Mountain in one of the lowest scoring games in district tournament history. In the preliminary game, Catasauqua's Ron Hassler scores 48 points, believed to be a district record, to lead the Rough Riders to a 72-71 overtime win over Bangor for the district's third Class B spot in the PIAA tournament. Hassler scores 29 of his points after intermission including five in overtime.

1974—After beating three league champions, including defending state champion Central Catholic, Notre Dame wins its first District 11 girls basketball championship with a 58-42 victory over Allen behind 27 points from sophomore Monica Williams.

1969—Ross Moore scores 24 points while Bob Racosky (12) and Danny Joseph (10) combine for 22, helping Dieruff (21-5) become the first team ever to win four straight District 11 Class A boys basketball titles with a 79-57 win over West Hazleton at the Harrisburg Farm Show Arena. The Huskies advance to meet unbeaten Penncrest in the PIAA tournament.

March 9

1984—Bidding for its first District 11 basketball championship since 1954, Northern Lehigh comes up short when the Bulldogs become Pottsville's 29th straight victim in a 60-54 loss to the Crimson Tide (31-1) in the Class 3A final at Stabler Arena.

1959—The Allentown School Board approves Dick Schmidt (basketball), Francis O'Keefe (football), Larry Fornicola (wrestling) and Dennis McGinley (swimming) as the first coaches at Dieruff, the city's second high school scheduled to open its doors for the first time in the fall.
