FROM THE CAR PARK TO A ZONE ROOKIE'S WINNER . . .

I Glover, Sunday, 30 August 2015

THE classic expression "it's not how you start, it's where you finish" would best describe this year's zone Rookie winner, Graeme Wong who may never have picked up a bowl if wasn't for the Mona Vale bowling club's car park.

"After I started parking my truck in the club's car park I decided I'd join the club and that's how my interest in bowl's started," said Graeme.

"Then I suggested to my son that we have a game and it wasn't long before I was hooked. As I work nights I'm now rolling up most days, which means I miss on some sleep, but with the encouragement and pointers I've had from other bowlers it's all been worth it".

Having played in a few pennant games in Mona Vale's no. 2 side has obviously put the polish on Graeme's game, which was evident in his shot selection and the type of questions he asked the marker. 

His opponent in the final was Neutral Bay's John Ziller, who's introduction to bowl's was also somewhat unconventional. Similar to many of the club's social members it was only a matter of time before one or other of the club's sporting pursuits sparked some interest.

John chose bowl's over tennis and similar to Graeme, it wasn't long before he also became hooked on the game, due too a large extent on the encouragement from some of the club's top bowlers.

"Club champion, Lyn Thompson has given me a tremendous amount of help with my game, and I'm fortunate to have been her partner when we won a section of the mixed pair's competition, and we will now go to the State play offs at Tuncurry later this year," said John.    

While Graeme jumped out to a 9-3 start and maintained the lead throughout the game, the final 17-8 score was no indication of the bowling prowess displayed by both players.

Obviously Graeme's narrow win in his quarter final game wasn't too draining, as it finished with both players on 16-all in the 17-up game and the jack in the ditch. With both bowls in question nearly a metre away, the umpire was called which saw Graeme win the measure by a matter of millimetres.
