The Westgate Las Vegas Superbook isn’t offering an over/under total on the air pressure of the footballs used in Super Bowl 49.

That’s one of the only parts of next Sunday’s game between the New England Patriots and Seattle Seahawks at University of Phoenix Stadium in Glendale, Ariz., that gamblers can’t wager on at the world’s largest sports book. The Superbook commenced Super Bowl prop-betting season locally Thursday night, posting approximately 350 wagers on its betting board.

Customers can risk money on everything from which team has more penalty yards to whether Seattle quarterback Russell Wilson will throw more completions than Washington Wizards guard John Wall scores points against the Raptors Sunday.

Scrutinizing the options should work as a respite from the nonstop discussion on the NFL’s finding that the Patriots used under-inflated footballs in last week’s 45-7 win over the Indianapolis Colts in the AFC Championship Game. The deflation scandal had no impact on the odds.

“I think it’s a big deal about nothing,” Superbook Manager Ed Salmons said. “It’s the Patriots and there’s nothing going on this week so the media is going crazy with it.”
