JCU Facts
Did You Know...
Sharing With His Friends
In the 1993 season, JCU soccer player Pete Duggan copmpiled 14 assists, which still remains as a school record. In his career from 1990-93, he had 40 assists.
First NCAA Appearance
In 2014, the JCU women's basketball team won their first-ever NCAA Tournament game by defeating Texas Lutheran, 74-62.
JCU Takes Advantage of OSU
The wrestling team has a 3-3 overall record vs. Ohio State.  The last time JCU squared off against this powerhouse was 1984.
And Celebration Begins
JCU women's basketball recorded their first ever win on Febrauary 8, 1972 against Baldwin Wallace, 25-16
Leland a Force on The Boards
In 1959, JCU men's basketball player Leland Hall grabbed 26 boards vs. Allegheny.
Unstoppable
Cindy Shumaker was the winner of the Cleveland Cavaliers' Rebounders Club Award as Northeast Ohio's top collegiate women's basketball player in 1993, leading her team to the first 20 win season that year. 
Perfect Attendance
In addition to earning NCAA Division III All-American honors and two OAC championships in '98 and '99, wrestler Jim Ayers never missed a dual meet as a Blue Streak.
Spahar's Stellar Career
From 2010-14, Missy Spahar set school records for ponts (1,901 pts), rebounds (912), and field goals (724).  She also captured the single-season scoring record with 680 points in the 2013-14 season.
Bedell Breaks Through
At the 1969 PAC Cross Country Championships, JCU's Dave Bedell delivered the first individual league championship. He placed 1st with a time of 21.17.
Wrestling Monster
Chris Connelly was a three-time national qualifier in wrestling at JCU. He won individual  OAC championships in 1992 and 1994, and also earned All - American status in 1993 and 1994.
It's Up and Good
In 1984, Joe Timko made a 52 yard field against Cansius, which is the longest field goal to date in the history of John Carroll football.
Baseball's first OAC Championship
In 2014, the baseball team captured its first-ever OAC title with a  6-3 win over their cross-town rival, Baldwin Wallace.
The First Ever Varsity Game
On January 20, 1920 St. Ignatius College defeated the Forest City Knights of Columbus, 29-17. This was the first varsity basketball contest in school history.
Extra Points Matter
In 2002, JCU kicker George Theofilos kicked a record of 10 PATs in the game against Otterbein.
Not Even Tired
Jason Barnett holds the Blue Streak's record for wrestling 47 matches in a single season. While Mark Hawald has the career record of wrestling 153 matches.
Del Busso Tallys Nine Points
In 1996, men's soccer player Adrian Del Busso recorded nine points against Capital.
First Win Ever for Volleyball
Women's volleyball brought home their first win ever on September 30, 1980 against Oberlin. The final score was 2-0.
Grove's Gals Have Their Day

1991 marked John Carroll's first OAC cross country title. Under head coach Grove Jewett, the quintet of Michelle Green, Joanna Tomazic, Susan Stukus, Lauren Colaizzi, and Julie Koscinski placed in the top 15.
The Running Men

In 1974, running back, Tim Barrett, led the NCAA Division III in rushing and set the school record for rushing with 1,409 yards. Barrett was the second Blue Streak to have his number retired.
From a Basket to a Bat
Women's basketball Hall of Famer, Audrey Warnock, was a four-time All-Presdients' Athletic Conference honoree and a three-time PAC Softball Player of the Year. 
Blue Streaks See Plate All Game
John Carroll's baseball team had 55 at bats against Youngstown State University on April 16, 1973.
Volleyball Entering the Hall of Fame
The 1993 women's volleyball team was the first women's team sport to be inducted into the Hall of Fame. The team went undefeated in conference regular season and won the program's first OAC tournament title. 
Ken Can Do
In his 1976-1977 campaign, basektball player Ken Rehmer shot 91.5% from the free throw line - which still ranks first in the history of JCU basketball.
Start of A Legacy
Leo Miller, a John Carroll Hall of Fame inductee, became JCU's first-ever track and field national champion in 1987, placing first in the high jump.
Mitolo Shoots His Way To Stardom
In 2014, Rocky Mitolo became JCU's first-ever shot put All-American after placing seventh in the nation at the Outdoor Track and Field Championships.
JCU On The National Stage
On March 20, 2004 JCU Men's Basketball team defeated Amherst 96-85 and brought home third-place in the NCAA Division III Championship, best in JCU basketball history.
The Lyons Den
In 1991, goalie Michael Lyons recorded nine shutouts through the first ten matches.  Lyons finished his JCU soccer career with 27 shutouts from 1991-1994, setting a school record.
K Machine
Brittany Danilov finished the 2011 season with 191 strikeouts, topping the all-time JCU softball strikeout list. Then, in the following season, beat her own record with 208 K's.
Breaking New Ground
Between 2000-01, the JCU Men's Swimming & Diving team broke seven school records, including five individual and two team relay records.
First to win 100 matches
Tom Bennett became the first wrestler to ever win 100 matches at John Carroll, finishing his career with 111 victories and becoming a three-time All-American and a two-time PAC champion.
Holding All The Aces
Over the course of her volleyball career, Mary Ann Montagne had 241 service aces from 1986-1989. Her efforts were recognized when she was inducted into the JCU Athletic Hall of Fame in 2010.
It's Raining Threes
In 2010, Michael Hartnett hit nine 3-pointers against Capital.  The next season, his teammate Joe Meyer, tied Hartnett's mark when he drained nine shots from beyond the arc against Muskingum.
Most Decorated
Jim Petkunas, the most decorated swimmer in JCU history, won All-OAC honors 27 times, was a 25-time OAC champion, nine of those were individual, he also won 16 OAC championships in the relay.
What a Career
From 1975-78, JCU wrestler, Jim Weir had the highest individual winning percentage in his career at .904. Career wise at JCU he posted a .930 winning percentage.  Finishing with a career record of 98-10-1.
2003-04 Season of Dreams
In the 2003-2004 season, the JCU men's basketball team set a school record with 27 wins.
JCU Women's Soccer Put on a Scoring Clinic

On October 3, 1996, the JCU women's soccer team netted 10 goals against Lake Erie College.
Making History
Stephanie Turner became JCU's first woman in school history to win a national title when she brought home the gold for the 3 meter dive in the 1999-2000 season.
16 feet
 In 1998, Matt Lemieux set the University's indoor pole vault record of 16'0". The same year he set the outdoor record of 16'8.75".
Netted Twenty-Five Three Pointers
In 1999, JCU's Women's basketball team dropped 25 three pointers on Otterbein.
Getting into the End Zone
In 1950, Carl Taseff scored 23 touchdowns in a single season along side of his teammate, Don Shula.
Lived behind the three point line
In the 1988-89 season, JCU Men's Basketball player, Craig Huffman scored 79 three point field goals.
Hall of Fame MVP
Luke Baum, a track and field Hall of Fame inductee, from 1983-1986 was a two-time national qualifier, two-time PAC MVP, he also won three PAC titles, one in long jump and two in triple jump.
Setting her Teammates Up
On October 17, 1992, Leslie Mahl accounted for 70 assists in a volleyball match against Muskingum.
NCAA Appearances
The Men's John Carroll basketball team has played in ten NCAA Division III Championship Tournaments: 1983, 1986, 1996, 1997, 1998, 1999, 2003, 2004, 2005, 2007, 2009 and 2010.
Men's Basketball Team Drops 137 Points

On November 18, 2005 the JCU Men's basketball team put up 137 points vs. Emory & Henry. Between both schools they combined for 264 points, Emory & Henry having scored 127 points.
Kit O'Shaughnessy Freshman of the Year
Kit O'Shaughnessy became JCU's first-ever, OAC Volleyball Freshman of the Year in 2013.
The Shula Years
In 1948, legend Don Shula accomplished the best rushing average in a season with 6.6 yards a carry for John Carroll.
Scaffidi Aplenty
Julie Scaffidi holds the women's soccer record of 46 goals over the course of her career (1997-2000). She also earned the OAC Offensive Player of the Year award in 2000.
Mackenzie at the Plate
In 2013, Mackenzie Griffin set JCU softball records for batting average (.548), hits (68), and doubles (20) .
Super Cooper
Beth Cooper set a record of 3,468 assists during her 4 year volleyball career at JCU (2000-04), and has the highest single-season fielding percentage mark when she did not commit an error as a catcher during the 2002 softball campaign.
Two Sport Hall of Famer
Thea Consler, inducted in to the Hall of Fame for both soccer, and track and field, was the first woman in JCU history to win All-American honors in track and field. She was a four-time All-American and four-time national qualifier in long jump, she was the MVP of the OAC four times and was also the MVP in soccer in 1996 when she won the Clyde A. Lamb award for most outstanding athlete in the OAC.
Dominating the OAC
Hall of Fame standout, Matt Lemieux, won the national championship in pole vault in 1998, during his illustrious career, Lemieux was a three-time All-American, a four-time national qualifier, an eight-time All-OAC performer, and a three-time OAC champion.
Puts up a Record Scoring Day
In 1979, the JCU women's basketball team put up 109 points and 52 field goals vs. Notre Dame College
Pinchak
Cyril Pinchak holds the school's all-time indoor track & field record for the mile and 3000m run. Pinchak also received All-American honors during his career as a Blue Streak.
Undefeated Legend
Rich Eslich is the only national champion in JCU wrestling history to go undefeated in his national championship run.
Arth Completes Passes at Will
On October 21, 2000, Tom Arth completed 42 of 62 passes against Ohio Northern. This is the most pass attempts and pass completions by a quarterback in John Carroll history.
Having His Day at the Charity Stripe
In 1952, JCU Men's Basketball player, George Dalton made 19 free throws from the charity stripe vs. Seton Hall.
Three-point Machine
Emilee Ritchie leads women's basketball in most three-point field goals made with eight against Mount Union on December 8, 2010.
A Rebounding Machine
In 1991, JCU women's basketball player, Cindy Shumaker grabbed 23 boards in a game vs. Muskingum.
Big Hops
Mike Minjock, a former All-American Track and Field athlete, holds the school record in the indoor long jump (23'10.25"), outdoor long jump (24'11.75"), stadium long jump (22'11.25"), as well as indoor triple jump (44'9.5") for John Carroll.
Shutting Out Opponents

From 1993-95, softball pitcher Angela Rochowiak had 17 shutouts throughout her career at JCU.
Doing the Dirty Work

Jim Peters, a 1972 graduate of John Carroll, holds the school's rebounding record.  Peters compiled 813 rebounds in his four-year career on the men's basketball team.
Mark It Eight

The men's basketball squad has been OAC champions eight times in program history.
Nets Twenty-Three Goals on the Year
In 1991, JCU men's soccer player Pablo Ramirez set the program record when he scored 23 goals in a single season.
A Legendary Coach
Tony DeCarlo, John Carroll's head wrestling coach from 1964 - 1987, led the Blue Streaks to their first NCAA Division III National Championship.
A Legend

Tony DeCarlo was the head wrestling coach from 1964-87. DeCarlo coached JCU to 21 consecutive PAC and 13 NCIT championships. 
Do Not Take Him For Grant-Ed
In a 1996 soccer match vs. Kenyon, the Blue Streaks goalkeeper Grant Mast set a school-record of 21 saves in the contest. Mast would go on to post eight shutouts in 1996, and was inducted into the JCU Hall of Fame in 2009.
Grzybowski at the Net
Beth Grzybowski leads women's volleyball with most kills in three sets at 24.
Base Crook
Audrey Warnock finished her softball career at JCU perfect in stolen bases. She went 32-32 in the 1987-88 season.
Wideikis and Recko Suit up
In 1998, baseball players Jim Wideikis and Craig Recko dressed for 43 games - setting a single-season record.
First NCAA Tournament Victory
The 1993 women's volleyball win against Guilford College in the first round of tournament play marked the first NCAA tournament victory in school history.
Taking Them By Storm
In the 2001-2002 season, swimmer Carrie Scherger helped to set a total of seven school records, including three in individual events and four others in team relays.
An Offensive Machine
QB Tom Arth threw 89 touchdown passes in his John Carroll career (1999-2002). In 2000 he threw for a remarkable 31 TD passes.
Academic All-American
In 2013, catcher MacKenzie Griffin was the Division III, Capital One Academic All-America® award winner for softball.
Running Free
In 1983,  Steve Brown held school records in four of the six freestyle swimming events. He set three of those records in the same season.
Record Breaking Blue Streak
Brian Unk, an All-American golfer for Carroll, made a name for himself on the Canadian Pro Tour. At the 2009 Seaforth Classic, Unk shot a final round 64 to win the tournament, doing so with a tour-record score of 256 strokes, and record-breaking 28 under par total.
Magyari Goes the Distance
In 1983 and '84 baseball player John Magyari had eight complete games in the two seasons combined.
Master Thief
Katie Benjamin set the women's basketball season steal record in the 2001-2002 season with 87.
Barnett for the Win
In the 1986-87 season, Wrestler, Jason Barnett set a school record for most victories in a season after coming out on top 40 times that season.
Under Seven
Raj Arasu set the John Carroll indoor track record in the 55-meter dash in 2009, clocking in at 6.54
Giant Killers
Throughout the years, JCU wrestling has defeated Notre Dame, Stanford, Cleveland State, Ohio State, Marquette, Kent State, Georgia Tech, Bowling Green and Central Florida.
Scoring Spahar
Melissa Spahar holds the women's basketball record for most points in one season at 680 in 27 games.
A Team To Remember
JCU's 1974-75 wrestling team was the first team to be inducted into the JCU Athletic Hall of Fame.
McDaniels Succeeding in the Pro's

Josh McDaniels, who played at JCU in the 90's, is the offensive coordinator for the New England Patriots. McDaniels has won three Super Bowls during his professional coaching career thus far.
