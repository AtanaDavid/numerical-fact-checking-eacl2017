Geography | History
Ecology | Culture

Culture

Jazz is the only musical form born in the United States.

Hawaii contains the only royal palace in the United States territory.

Hollywood is the movie capital of the world.

The United States has the world's largest economy, and the world's largest national debt.

Since the 1870s, baseball has been called the American Pastime.

Hispanic people are now the largest ethnic minority in the United States.

Ecology

The American Bald Eagle is no longer considered an endangered species.

Millions of buffalo once roamed the Great Plains.

the 1989 Exxon Valdez oil spill was the worst in US history.

Opossums are the only marsupials native to the United States.

Grizzly bears can run as fast as 35mph.

The Three Mile Island Nuclear Facility had the worst nuclear disaster in U.S. history.

Earth Day was first created and celebrated in the U.S. in 1970.

Geography

The Great Lakes are the largest group of freshwater lakes on Earth.

The Mississippi river basin covers about 40% of the United States.

Mt. Mitchell, in North Carolina, is the highest point east of the Mississippi.

The Grand Canyon is one of the seven natural wonders of the world.

The Rocky Mountains contain some of the highest peaks in North America.

The Great Plains cover 25% of North America.

Alaska is the westernmost US state.

Yellowstone National Park is the first National Park in the world.

History

In 1779, there were more Americans fighting in the British Army than with the Continental Army.

The chance of surviving a wound in the Civil War was 1 in 7.

4000 Cherokee Indians died on the Trail of Tears.

Between 1929 and 1932 during the Great Depression, the income of the average American family was cut almost in half.

The Montgomery, Alabama bus boycott lasted for 382 days.

The Los Alamos Nuclear Testing facility was bought for $440,000.

The Apollo program successfully put the first man on the Moon.

The United States nearly doubled in size with the Louisiana Purchase.

The US paid pirates over $100,000,000 in ransom between 1785 and 1800.
