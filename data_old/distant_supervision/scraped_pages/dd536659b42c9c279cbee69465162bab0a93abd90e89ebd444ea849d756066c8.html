28 July ·

Hi everyone!

It is time to introduce the organisation we work with! Last year Wale Wale initiated a collaboration with us. Through this collaboration Wale Wale is provided with engineering students from Chalmers that may present feasible sustainable solutions.

Wale Wale is an NGO located at the edge of one of Africa’s biggest slum areas, Kibera. Unique in the aspect that the organisation is built upon the involvement of the youths who play important roles both in the everyda...y work as well as in managing the organisation. The Wale Wale Youth Center is the heart of their work in Kibera. The center is open whenever the youths are free – evenings, weekends and school holidays, coming to hang around with their friends, study, eat and develop their creative talents. Common activities are dance, music and film production.

Wale Wales vision is to continue their current work but in a much larger scale. In order to do that, Wale Wale will need a new facility. Last autumn, Chalmers architect student Linda Eriksson did her thesis for Wale Wale, designing the Wale Wale Future Center. WWFC should be large enough to welcome more members, have services good enough to attract youth and the building itself should be impressive enough to communicate that it is important to invest in the young generation of Kibera and Kenya as a whole. Being located in an area where access to water and electricity is limited, the building should also be as self-sufficient as possible. The ambition is to set an example for future building projects in development countries. This is where Chalmers students once more comes into the picture.

Don't forget to like Wale Wale Kenya or visit their homepage https://walewalekenya.com/ (:

2 weeks ago, we had the final day of the lecture series "Integration for Engineers".
