Story

THE TOUR: On 9th June 2006, I will join a 5-man team on a two week pedal from Land's End to John O'Groats. Despite being uphill the whole way..., there will be no rest days, we will carry all our kit and will face everything the British Summer has to throw at us; averaging 68 miles per day.

THE ROUTE

Day 1 - 10 June - Lands End to Par ( Cornwall ) - 56 miles

Day 2 - 11 June - Par to Moretonhampstead (Cornwall/Devon) - 55 miles

Day 3 - 12 June - Mortonhampstead to Bridgwater (Devon/Somerset) - 59 miles Day 4 - 13 June - Bridgwater to Stroud (Somerset/Gloucestershire) - 65 miles Day 5 - 14 June - Stroud to Ironbridge (Gloucestershire/Shropshire) - 74 miles Day 6 - 15 June - Ironbridge to Warrington (Shropshire/Cheshire) - 62 miles Day 7 - 16 June - Warrington to Kirkby Lonsdale (Cheshire/Lancashire) - 64 miles Day 8 - 17 June - Kirkby Lonsdale to Gretna (Lancashire/Dumfries & Galloway) - 89 miles Day 9 - 18 June - Gretna to Cumnock (Dumfries & Galloway/Ayrshire) - 73 miles Day 10 - 19 June - Cumnock to Ardlui (East Ayrshire/Argyll & Bute) - 86 miles Day 11 - 20 June - Ardlui to Spean Bridge (Argyll & Bute/Highland) - 68 miles Day 12 - 21 June - Spean Bridge to Dingwall ( Highland ) - 63 miles Day 13 - 22 June - Dingwall to Helmsdale ( Highland ) - 66 miles Day 14 - 23 June - Helmsdale to John O'Groats - 51 miles

THE CHARITIES

In completing 'LEJOG', I hope to raise funds for two worthy causes:

ACTIONAID CAMBODIA: I would like to continue the good work of a close schoolmate called ADAM COLE who was tragically killed in a car accident on 19th April 2006. He had just sponsored a 9-year old girl called Ya Chea. She lives in the  Banteay Meanchey province of Cambodia. This province is one of the regions in which Actionaid has set up a variety of community projects to help the poorest and most disadvantaged people. If you would like to sponsor me and make a contribution to Actionaid Cambodia, please use this webpage. More details will follow highlighting how and where the donations will be spent.

NBCS: One of the most rewarding parts of this tour will be the stunning scenery surrounding us as we clock up the miles. Being able to clearly see the breathtaking views is something that I do not take for granted. Therefore, I would like to complete this challenge and see as much of Britain as possible on behalf of those who would love to but are not yet in a position to do so. The funds I manage to raise will go towards helping the blind and partially sighted children feel the same sense of achievement as they reach their own recreational goals. If you would like to sponsor me and make a contribution to the NBCS, please click on the following link: www.justgiving.com/TOMWATSONCYCLE.

THANK YOU: Both charities would be delighted to receive any donations you feel you would like to make so a big thank you in advance. Your donation will also be an incentive for me to keep grinding out the miles when the rain is horizontal, the chain has come off again, I have punctures in both tyres, there is no reception for World Cup updates and my saddle continues to slowly slice me in two!

FINALLY

We aim to sample only the local cuisine as we tick off the miles (Cornish pasties, Lancashire hotpot etc - please place all suggestions in comment boxes below) and reach each checkpoint in time for all World Cup games.

We already have a number of people keen and committed to join us for various legs of the tour. If you like the sound of this, please feel free to catch up with us at any point along the way. 

Please check out the justgiving pages of those committed to completing the challenge with me: www.justgiving.com/victourswithhugo and www.justgiving.com/teambusby

Donating through this site is simple, fast and totally secure. It is also the most efficient way to sponsor me: National Blind Childrens Society will receive your money faster and, if you are a UK taxpayer, an extra 28% in tax will be added to your gift at no cost to you.
