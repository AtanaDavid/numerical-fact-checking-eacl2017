Novak Djokovic out of Wimbledon 2016: World No.1 suffers stunning loss to Sam Querrey

NOVAK DJOKOVIC had his bid for a calendar-year Grand Slam finished today after he suffered a shocking loss to American Sam Querrey.

By Tony Stenson / Published 2nd July 2016

GETTY

STUNNED: Djokvic had his worst Wimbledon result in 10 years

Novak Djokovic’s march to become a true tennis legend ended after 24 hours of rain, sun, delays – and ultimately tears.

His dream of joining the pantheon of greats was blocked by American Sam Querrey who serves with a venom that destroys contenders.

Querrey rattled down 31 aces at an average of 131mph, turning the defending champion into jelly.

Make no mistake. This is a tennis shocker.

Querrey is America’s No.4 but is ranked 41 in the world. It should have been a shoo-in for a bloke who holds four Grand Slams at the same time.

Djokovic arrived at Wimbledon unbeaten in 30 Grand Slam matches and a short price to win all four major titles in the same year for the first time since Aussie great Rod Laver did it in 1969.

    But he looked so rattled at one stage he called for his coach Boris Becker to try to dig him out of a hole. He could not.

    The Monte Carlo-based Serb restarted his rain-interrupted match two sets down and won the third set 6-3.

    But with the crowd favouring the underdog, Querrey continued to serve big and edged a fourth set tie-break 7-5.

    Djokovic got to 2-2 and 4-4 in the fourth set but then fluffed his lines.

    He said: “I will come back stronger. I’ve a family and a life outside tennis and now need a rest.”

    Querrey said: “I played the break points really well. I was concerned he might come out after being behind but I was ready for that.

    “He is on his way to being the best ever but I am not even sure who I will play next.

    “Let me enjoy this one first!”

    Djokovic added: “Congratulations to Sam. He played terrific. Part of his game was brutal. Well done. He just overpowered me.

    “I believe in positive things in life. I managed to win four Grand Slams in a row in two different seasons and I prefer to think of that rather than failure.

    “I had chances in the fourth set but I wasn’t feeling the ball as usual and I don’t want to take anything away from my opponent.

    “He played really well.

    “It is not necessary to talk about rain delays because it was the same for both of us and frustrating for the fans.

    “My opponent played a very high level and he deserved to win. He has been around for many years and is a good player.

    “How will this affect me? We shall see. I will move on from this a stronger player.”
