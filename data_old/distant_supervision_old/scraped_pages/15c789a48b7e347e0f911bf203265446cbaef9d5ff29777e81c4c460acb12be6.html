Top-seeded Gators gymnasts look to capture elusive national championship

By Edgar Thompson, Orlando Sentinel, April 18, 2013
GAINESVILLE - As the scoreboard confirmed what she already knew, Florida Gators gymnastics coach Rhonda Faehn could do the math. A stumble, a missed landing and all the little things that are so costly in the end left her Gators just .075 of a point from their first NCAA championship. Alabama's victory over Florida and third-place UCLA in 2012 was the closest competition in meet history. This week, Faehn feels top-ranked Florida has what it takes to finally get over the hump, the high bar, the vault and anything else that stands in its way. "I feel like in years past the teams might have said we can do this, maybe half of them believe it, maybe half of them thought, 'This might be kind of hard,'" Faehn said.

Orlando shop dresses Miss America, top 2 runners-up

By Sandra Pedicini, Orlando Sentinel, January 24, 2013
Mallory Hytes Hagan won the Miss America crown two weeks ago, but when it comes to pageant gowns, Regalia Magnificent Attire absolutely ruled the runway. Hagan and the first and second runners-up all wore dresses that came from the downtown Orlando boutique. In all, Regalia outfitted 16 contestants in evening wear this year, the most ever in the store's 24-year history. "We're walking away with just the biggest prize ever. To get one, two and three was just fantastic," co-owner David Lang said.

Triplett, Houston runners up for leadership after George Zimmerman shot Trayvon Martin

January 20, 2013
Jeff Triplett and Valarie Houston: Racial tensions were running hot in Sanford after neighborhood watchman George Zimmerman shot Trayvon Martin. Cool heads emerged in the person of Triplett, Sanford's mayor, and Houston, pastor at Allen Chapel. Triplett remained calm, reasonable and, importantly, respectful as he faced the national media and huge crowds. Houston, meanwhile, advocated a message of peace and love, while also advocating for justice. Both were determined to help their community get through a difficult and painful time.

Stan Van Gundy a Central Floridian of the Year runner-up for leading a succesful campagin

January 20, 2013
Stan Van Gundy: After being sacked as coach of the Orlando Magic, he could have been forgiven for hiding inside his home in Lake Mary. Instead, Van Gundy stepped out as leader of a campaign to raise property taxes in Seminole County to support the district's top-notch but cash-strapped public schools. With two kids of his own in the system, he wasn't willing to watch the schools struggle without doing something. He made speeches, did debates, even waved signs on street corners for the cause.

Harriett Lake a Central Floridian of the Year runner-up for her generosity

January 20, 2013
Harriett Lake: We should all have her sense of humor, and at least a sliver of her generosity. Each year Lake gives in the neighborhood of $1.5 million to a variety of charities, many of them aiding kids and the arts. And get this: Each year she goes to a local Walmart and pays off the toys that are on layaway. Imagine the joy of struggling parents who learn a guardian angel is watching out for them. We were awash in nominations for Lake, a testimony to the Wizard of Oz's observation that "a heart is not judged by how much you love; but by how much you are loved by others.

Thune, Portman, Pawlenty, VP runners-up, take convention stage

By Lisa Mascaro and This post has been corrected. , August 29, 2012
TAMPA, Fla. – On the night that Paul D. Ryan was making his debut at the Republican convention, the stage was given to three vice presidential runners-up. Republican Sen. John Thune of South Dakota was first up among the three, followed a little later by Sen. Rob Portman of Ohio and former Gov. Tim Pawlenty of Minnesota. They were among those reported to have been under consideration for the VP spot. Their appearances provided a view of the top tier of the GOP – and the running-mate choices available to Mitt Romney . Thune kicked off the speeches with a stately, if serious, critique of President Obama – particularly the administration's approach to regulations on clean air and water that Republicans say are excessive and have stunted economic growth.
