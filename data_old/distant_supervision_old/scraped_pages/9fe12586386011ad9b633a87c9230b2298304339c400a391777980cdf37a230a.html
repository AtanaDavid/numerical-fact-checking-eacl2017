Golf Courses by Seve Ballesteros

1 Comment
 10 May 2011   Posted by Rory

 Loading ...


Seve Ballesteros is best known for his creative genius and unrivalled imagination on the golf course. His ability to conjure magic from the most impossible spots and turn certain bogeys into birdies is unparalleled in the history of golf. After a more than successful playing career which saw him win 5 major championships and set countless records along the way, this imagination and creative talent was turned to golf course design. Having successfully completed numerous projects all over the world, we take thought it fitting to pay tribute to the part of his legacy that is often over looked largely due to his sheer brilliance as a player. Here are 5 of his finest creations.

Golf de Pont Royal – France

Built in 1991, the course at Pont Royal is situated on a vast plateau that overlooks the gorgeous Durance valley. This course sits in an enclosed 180 hectare estate and features generous fairways that set up nicely for you to open your shoulders and have a rip with the driver. Unsurprisingly, Seve designed the undulating greens to test even the best putters in the game. Pont Royal is a beautifully maintained course that features subtle nuances that are designed to test the player’s tactical and technical proficiency. The course is one of the best courses in Europe and is the only French golf course that bears the signature of the great Spaniard.

Alhaurin Golf Resort – Spain

The Seve Ballesteros designed course at Alhaurin is widely regarded as one of the best in the area around Marbella. Located in the Sierra de Mijas, the course has spectacular mountain views and is a thorough test of golf from tee to green. Measuring 6,801 yards from the tips, Alhaurin demands length from the tee but not at the expense of accuracy. Some of the fairways have severe undulations and are marshalled by mature pines and water hazards that wait menacingly to gobble up any stray drives. Shrewdly placed bunkers and clever run off areas demand precision from approach shots…unless you have a bit of Seve in you! The course is regularly chosen to host local and regional tournaments, which is testament to the quality of the Ballesteros design. This course is a must on any Costa Del Sol golf break.

Warning – Pack plenty of golf balls!

Westerwood – Scotland

Westerwood is one of Scottish golf’s best kept secrets. Seve designed it with the help if Dave Thomas so it comes as no surprise that it is such a high quality golf course. Now the subject of a development programme designed to improve drainage and conditioning, the par-72 layout is stretched through silver birches and firs, and the heathland and heather that are so typical of Scotland. There are many notable holes at Westerwood but one of Seve’s is the signature hole. ‘The Waterfall Hole’ is a par three with an elevated tee and a green surrounded by a 60-foot rocky face and a devilish little burn that sweeps across the front of the bunker. Not only is the layout a cracker, Seve’s Scottish creation boasts offers stunning views of the nearby Kilsyth and Campsie hills.

Quinta do Vale – Portugal

Seve called on his considerable imagination and sense of creativity when designing Quinta do Vale. This fantastic course features an unusual but heady blend of 6 par 3’s, 6 par 4’s and 6 par 5’s. Opened for play in the summer of 2008, this course is an impressive modern layout which benefits from extraordinary views of the Guadiana River. Seve set out to design a course that would test players of all abilities and his Algarve layout certainly doesn’t disappoint. Featuring perfectly manicured fairways and greens that will set the pulse racing over those pesky little three footers, Quinta do Vale is a must on any Eastern Algarve golf break.


Peraleja – Spain
Peraleja is one of Seve’s youngest creations but the course has matured remarkably quickly in a short space of time. Taking into account the designers considerable imagination, it is no surprise that this course features more than one stand out hole. The courses main defence is left to the large lakes that patiently await any drives that wonder too far off course and the winds that rush in from the nearby Murcia coastline.  As you would expect from a short game genius, Peraleja tests a player’s ability on and around the greens. This is another Ballesteros design that provides a testing but enjoyable round of golf to players of all abilities.
