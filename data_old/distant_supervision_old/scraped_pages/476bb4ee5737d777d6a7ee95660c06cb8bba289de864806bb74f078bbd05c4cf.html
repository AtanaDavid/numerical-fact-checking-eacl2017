Di Layzelle nominated for Teacher of the Year Award!

13 January 2016

 Croydon College’s Di Layzelle MBE is nominated for “Teacher of the Year Award” at the Croydon Champions Awards. The awards, now in their 16th year, highlight the remarkable stories of the borough’s residents who do astonishing things to help others.

The aim of the Croydon Champions awards is to give them the recognition that they deserve. For the ‘Teacher of the Year award’, the Judges are looking for a teacher who has made a real difference going above and beyond their job description.

Di, who was recently awarded an MBE for her exceptional work at the college, completely dedicates herself to ensuring students excel. She has helped encourage students to engage with their community whilst also helping students' to improve their essential employability skills and progression. Thus building on the learners’ confidence and skills and preparing them for University and the workplace.

Di’s work was officially recognised in Croydon College’s last Ofsted inspection, "The college has extremely strong focus on the very good development of students' personal and employability skills through its outstanding volunteering initiatives and many other projects, which have significant impact on the local communities in which the students reside.”

Di’s altruistic dedication to learners is apparent when talking to students at Croydon College. Unanimously they all agreed with Gloria Nsiah, said “Di sees things in people they don’t see in themselves, she encourages us to be the best we can be in all that we put our minds to.”

Di’s “Can-do” attitude is infectious when in her company. Student Soraia Barbosa adds, “Di supports everyone and always provides opportunities to develop and build professional and personal skills. She always has a smile on her face and is a huge ball of positive energy.”

On hearing the news of her nomination, Di said, “Working with so many amazing young people is an absolute privilege so it is an honour to be nominated for this prestigious award.”

Good luck Di! The College has the confidence in a win!
