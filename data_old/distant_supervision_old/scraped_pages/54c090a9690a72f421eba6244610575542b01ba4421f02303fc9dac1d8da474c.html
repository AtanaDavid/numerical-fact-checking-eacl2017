DAVID MARSHALL'S SECOND RESERVE WIN


DAVID MARSHALL, 2016 RESERVE SINGLE'S WINNER
Photograph:IAN GLOVER
I Glover, Monday, 11 July 2016

                

           DAVID'S LONG DAY AT THE OFFICE  

DAVID MARSHALL won his first Reserve singles while at Dee Why five years ago, then after a long U.K. holiday came back and was a runner up in 2014. After moving house and due to it's proximity, he joined the Balgowlah club and decided to see if he could reproduce the same form as his first win.

The jury's still out on the form aspect, but he certainly had to produce good bowls in his morning semi final against Belrose's John Treadwell to only just snatch a two shot victory.

The much travelled Tony Chapman was his opponent in the final after his comfortable and quicker 31-14 win over Willoughby Park's Peter Psaltis, and then forced to have an extended lunch break due to David's prolonged semi final.

This final was in total contrast in every respect to the adjacent rinks, where the players were either totally engrossed, perfectly relaxed or possibly convinced they could be somewhere else doing something more constructive.  

An extraordinary low pitched wailing filtered across the green, particularly when the scoreboard showed 21-all, after Tony had led 17-9. 

The wailing was replaced with loud sounds of anguish, together with disjointed conversations with a person or persons unknown, as there were no spectators watching that game.

While David increased his lead to 26, Tony had produced some good bowls to reach 27, then it was David's turn and he played some impressive shots to go out a 31-27 winner.
