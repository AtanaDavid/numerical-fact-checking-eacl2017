Perceptions in Engineering

Bury Grammar School Girls Science and Maths staff and some Year 9 students have been involved in a research project being run by the Institute of Mechanical Engineers into the perception of engineering within schools. Staff and students were interviewed and took part in an on line survey. The schools involvement will be acknowledged in the final report which will aim to suggest ways of raising awareness of the importance of young people pursuing engineering careers. The researchers from Sheffield Hallam University were very impressed by our school and very grateful for our input.

23 October ·

Author Visit - Krystal Sutherland

We were incredibly lucky to have a visit from talented debut author Krystal Sutherland at the end of this half term. Girls in Years 8 & 9 were treated to a question and answer session with the author followed by a mammoth book signing.

Our Chemical Hearts is Krystal’s first book and she spoke generously about the writing and editing process that led to its publication. Compared to hugely successful writers such as John Green, Rainbow Rowell a...nd Jennifer Niven, Krystal told girls of the early success of her book (book rights sold in 21 countries as well as a film adaptation in development) and spoke eloquently about the necessity of writing truthfully for her teenage audience.

Though only in its second week of publication, if the queue for book signing is anything to go off, Our Chemical Hearts is bound to be a huge success. I hope that girls enjoy reading it over the half term holidays.
