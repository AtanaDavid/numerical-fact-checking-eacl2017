BBC's John Inverdale accused of 'ignoring' Anthony Joshua during 'disrespectful' interview

BBC's John Inverdale 'ignores' Anthony Joshua during Olympic interview Play! 00:26
  • Callum Davis
18 August 2016 • 10:07pm

John Inverdale has received renewed online criticism after he was accused of ignoring British heavyweight boxer Anthony Joshua.

The veteran broadcaster has come under fire on a number of separate occasions during the Olympic games, leading to a number of observers calling the level of scrutiny unfair.

Inverdale, 58, was talking to Joshua following the culmination of the women's boxing semi-final involving Great Britain's Nicola Adams.

The presenter was discussing Adams' chances of retaining her Olympic title when her opponent in the final, France's Sarah Ourahmoune, walked past the camera.

As Joshua previewed Adams' Olympic final, Inverdale appeared to ignore the London 2012 gold medal winner, while he tried to catch the French fighter's eye for an impromptu interview.

John Inverdale listen intently as he interviews Anthony Joshua pic.twitter.com/u7D8nasWAa

— reuben (@reubeern) 18 August 2016

Joshua, who remains unbeaten since turning professional following his 2012 Olympic triumph, is in Rio as a part of BBC's Olympic coverage of Great Britain's boxing hopefuls.

Viewers were quick to spot Inverdale's distracted demeanour, accusing the presenter of showing Joshua a lack of respect.

How rude is John inverdale? Talking to Anthony Joshua but ignoring him and constantly looking anywhere but at him! #bbcrio2016

— Martyn Scott (@Martyns5) August 18, 2016

Joshua appeared to pause on a number of occasions while Inverdale tried in vain to talk to the Olympic finalist.

While Joshua refrained from following Steve Redgrave's example and storming off camera, many viewers commented that the World Heavyweight champion looked far from pleased with Inverdale's interview technique.

Anthony Joshua looks like he wants to knock out John Inverdale.

— Sean Clark (@BrazilianSean) August 18, 2016

The BBC presenter has been the subject of a number of online attacks following several minor gaffes during his Olympic coverage.

The interview with Joshua comes just a week after Inverdale was accused of ‘everyday sexism’ after ignoring the Olympic achievements of Serena and Venus Williams.

Andy Murray corrects John Inverdale gaffe about women tennis players Play! 00:24

Inverdale was corrected by Great Britain's Andy Murray when he congratulated the two-time Olympic champion on becoming "the  first person to ever win two Olympic tennis gold medals."

“Well to defend the singles title, I think Venus and Serena have won about four each," Murray grinned.
