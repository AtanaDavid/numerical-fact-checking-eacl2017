Celebrating Marie Curie's 149th Birthday

Marie Sklodowska Curie was born in Poland on 7 November 1867. She is the first person to have been awarded two Nobel Prizes. In 1903 she received her first, in Physics.

More about Marie Curie

A Fruitful Collaboration

Marie married Pierre Curie in 1895. They started collaborating, studied radioactive substances and discovered two new elements - polonium and radium.

Read more about the Curies and their work

Awarded a Second Time

After her husband's death, Marie Curie continued her research. In 1911 she was awarded her second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry, in recognition of her work in radioactivity.

Watch a short video clip of Marie Curie in her lab

They Revealed the Secrets of Exotic Matter

The Nobel Prize in Physics 2016 was divided, one half awarded to David J. Thouless, the other half jointly to F. Duncan M. Haldane and J. Michael Kosterlitz "for theoretical discoveries of topological phase transitions and topological phases of matter".

2016 Nobel Peace Prize

The Norwegian Nobel Committee has decided to award the Nobel Peace Prize for 2016 to Colombian President Juan Manuel Santos for his resolute efforts to bring the country's more than 50-year-long civil war to an end, a war that has cost the lives of at least 220 000 Colombians and displaced close to six million people.

In Memoriam

Dario Fo Passes Away

Amused, engaged and provided perspectives

Dario Fo was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature 1997 with the motivation "who emulates the jesters of the Middle Ages in scourging authority and upholding the dignity of the downtrodden".

Biography
1997 Nobel Prize in LIterature

Shimon Peres 1923-2016

Shimon Peres passed away on 28 September at age 93. He was awarded the 1994 Nobel Peace Prize together with Yasser Arafat and Yitzhak Rabin "for their efforts to create peace in the Middle East".

James W. Cronin Passes Away

James W. Cronin passed away on 25 August, 84 years old. He was awarded the 1980 Nobel Prize in Physics for the discovery that the matter-antimatter symmetry is violated when the neutral K-meson decays.

Chemistry Laureate Roger Tsien Dies, Age 64

Roger Tsien shared the 2008 Nobel Prize in Chemistry "for the discovery and development of the green fluorescent protein, GFP". He passed away on 24 August.
