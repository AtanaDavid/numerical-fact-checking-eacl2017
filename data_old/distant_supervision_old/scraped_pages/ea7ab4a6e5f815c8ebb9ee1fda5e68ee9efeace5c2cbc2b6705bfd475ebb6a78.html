Centre tackles ‘dodgy’ work practices that exploit migrants
June 24, 2016

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

The Wembley-based Work Rights Centre (WoRC) has produced a four-step guide to employment rights for migrant and UK-born workers in ‘precarious work’. Precarious work is typically characterised by poor pay, lack of protection and no security.

WoRC relies on a team of dedicated volunteers who have been trained in employment rights. They do not provide legal advice and cannot represent people in court. Instead the volunteer team helps workers understand their employment rights, improve their employability, and take every step necessary before formal legal action begins.

Immigration rise balances birth rate drop says ONS
June 23, 2016

 

 

Rising immigration compensated for the Britain’s falling birth rate last year, with the population growing by half a million to 65.1 million. The Office for National Statistics said the 513,000 or 0.8% rise in the number of people living in Britain in June 2015 was in line with average annual rises over the last decade.

Migrants have little impact on pay rates says Low Pay Commission
June 23, 2016

In a letter to the TUC General Secretary, Frances O’Grady, the chair of the Low Pay Commission, Sir David Norgrove says: “the share of immigrants in the workforce has had little or no impact on the pay rates of the indigenous population”.

Norgove writes that older Low Pay Commission research and more recent studies reach the same broad conclusion that “the effect of immigration on pay is limited” equating to a drop in annual pay rates of about 1 penny an hour on a pay rate of £8 an hour for workers in the low-paid sector.

The letter goes on to say that the big threat to wages is economic slump after a possible Brexit vote.

Channel-walking refugee given nine-month jail sentence in Refugee Week
June 22, 2016

Right in the middle of Refugee Week 2016, a Sudanese man has been sentenced to nine months in jail after walking through the Channel Tunnel from France to Britain.

Abdul Rahman Haroun, who has been granted asylum in the UK, pleaded guilty to "obstructing an engine or a carriage using a railway".

 

 

The 40-year-old Sudanese refugee was caught near the end of the 31-mile tunnel at Folkestone, Kent on 4 August last year.
