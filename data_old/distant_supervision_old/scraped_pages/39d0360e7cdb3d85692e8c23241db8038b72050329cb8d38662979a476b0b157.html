January 15, 2016

Community-Wide College Mentoring Partnership Addresses Student Retention

Kate Elliott, CYC Communications and Marketing Specialist
(513) 363-5251 (office); (513) 919-5710 (mobile) kelliott@cycyouth.org (email)

JANUARY – At a news conference Thursday, January 14, several prominent community organizations announced their collective response to the growing concern that too many college students drop out before earning a degree. Cincinnati Youth Collaborative (CYC), Cincinnati Public Schools (CPS), Cincinnati Scholarship Foundation (CSF), University of Cincinnati (UC), University of Cincinnati Blue Ash (UCBA), Accenture, and The Business of Good Foundation have all pledged their support to a new initiative which supports local college students to ensure their success.

The initiative comes at a time when the city is seeking solutions to the childhood poverty crisis. Speaking at the press conference, President & CEO of CYC, Jane Keller, reminded attendees, “More than 47% of our city’s children are living in poverty.” Citing education as a tool for breaking generational poverty, the initiative is built around mentoring, which Keller described as “a proven tool” for improving academic outcomes.

Keller continued, “Research shows that mentored youth are statistically more likely to enroll in college, more likely to have strong attendance, and more likely to have an overall positive attitude about school.”

The Business of Good Foundation, an organization from Cleveland working in Cincinnati for the first time, is providing its web-based mentoring platform. When first introduced in Cleveland, the platform supported more than 700 matches in the first three years. CYC will match college-bound CPS graduates with local mentors, and the relationships will be supported by a CYC Mentor Coordinator, and also by the resources and communication options made available in the online mentoring platform.

While the initiative will support college students at any local institution, Dean Cady Short-Thompson of UCBA and Dr. Santa Ono of UC were both present to pledge their support to the partnership and the students. Also present was CPS Superintendent Mary Ronan, who described the initiative as “a perfect fit with Cincinnati Public Schools’ My Tomorrow initiative.”

During a Q&A session at the end of the conference, Keller laid out the goal of 40 new college mentors by the beginning of the 2016-2017 school year

December 15, 2015

New CPS Online Magnet Lottery Results Show High Demand

Contact: Janet Walsh, Director, CPS Public Affairs: (513) 363-0023, 207-8181, walshja@cps-k12.org

District Announces Plans to Increase Seats at Preschool, Kindergarten

Cincinnati Public Schools leaders today announced that demand for CPS magnet schools rose significantly after the district switched from the traditional first-come, first-served application process to an online lottery application system. At a news conference, Superintendent Mary Ronan said overall demand rose by more than 500 applications — from 1200 to 1704 — for enrollment in CPS magnet schools for the 2016-2017 school year, mostly at the preschool and kindergarten grade levels.

In response, Ronan said, the district will expand preschool and kindergarten seats to meet as much of the demand as possible.

“Parents, as our customers, have spoken through this process, and we think that we have a responsibility to be responsive to our customers’ demands,” she said.

By adding additional classrooms at existing CPS magnet schools where space permits and by creating additional capacity at other locations, the district will be able to boost its ability to meet the demand, said CPS Chief Information Officer Sarah Trimble-Oliver, whose technology team designed the online lottery system that was conducted over five weeks from October 24 through November 29. With expansion at preschool, the district’s ability to meet demand will rise from 23 percent to 84 percent, and kindergarten expansion will increase the district’s ability to meet demand from 63 percent to 83 percent, she said.

Ronan said the ways in which the district will be able to meet more of the preschool demand include renovating and reopening as an early learning center a school the district owns on Vine Street in Over-the-Rhine. Up to 10 preschool classes could be created at that location; students would move on to their magnet school spots secured through the lottery when they entered kindergarten.

Deputy Superintendent Laura Mitchell also discussed plans to enhance the district’s My Tomorrow college and career readiness initiative next school year and work with schools and neighborhoods beginning in January on plans to improve neighborhood schools.

“I’m very excited to be able to share that we will begin dialoging with schools and community leaders about potential programs to strengthen neighborhood schools,” she said.

September 14, 2015

CPS Joins Districts Across Ohio in Releasing its Quality Profile Defining the Educational Value of its Schools

Today, Cincinnati Public Schools joined more than 50 other school districts in Ohio in releasing its Quality Profile, which outlines the district’s effectiveness beyond standardized testing measures. Titled, “Measuring What Matters,” Cincinnati Public Schools’ profile offers a snapshot of the district’s ‘whole child’ approach to preparing students for success in high school and beyond.

“You cannot determine a student’s potential simply based on test scores,” said Mary Ronan, CPS superintendent. “Our profile outlines how we work to prepare our students for success in the real world, beginning when they first arrive in our elementary buildings and continuing through high school. Our goal is to ensure that, by the time they graduate, we have prepared them to succeed not just academically, but to also have the confidence, tenacity and character it takes to make a difference in the world around them.”

“Measuring What Matters” details success with early literacy, as well as the strategies employed through the district’s My Tomorrow initiative for 5th- through 12th-graders that helps students graduate with a clear path to college and the careers they choose. Highlights include the district’s use of blended learning to expand Advanced Placement courses, the award-winning breakfast and lunch program that provides the nutrition students need to thrive in the classroom, the many services to support English Language Learners and the district’s award-winning Community Learning Centers that feature 21 school-based health centers.

The Quality Profile can be found on the district’s website, cps-k12.org.

August 27, 2015

CPS ACT Scores Climbing

Cincinnati Public Schools’ students are graduating with higher ACT scores, indicating a higher level of college readiness.

CPS’ district composite ACT score rose to 19.1 for the Class of 2015 — up from 18.5 for the Class of 2014 and the highest since 2011. The ACT is a college-entrance exam that tests students on knowledge in English, mathematics, reading and science.

This 19.1 composite score covers only CPS students who graduated in 2015. CPS gives the ACT to all 11th-graders, with the district covering the cost, to ensure students are focused on college preparation.

“We are very pleased to see our ACT composite scores going up,” said Superintendent Mary Ronan. “Our goal is that we continue to see improvement, as our new My Tomorrow initiative touches more students. We see My Tomorrow as a bold vision for education that combines higher expectations, technology and mentoring to prepare all our students for success after graduation.”

My Tomorrow, which began for 7th and 8th grades in 2014-15, now is expanding to grades 5-12. The initiative’s aim is that all CPS seventh-graders graduate within six years prepared to actively pursue their chosen career paths.

April 23, 2015

National Search Launched for SCPA Executive Director

Cincinnati Public Schools today launched a national search for the new position of Executive Director for the School for Creative and Performing Arts, a renowned K-12 arts school located in Over-the-Rhine.

Long considered one of the city’s educational and artistic gems, SCPA is located in the Erich Kunzel Center for Arts and Education, a $72 million facility named for the late Maestro Kunzel, whose vision led to the construction of the arts school through a unique private-public partnership.

The district’s decision to create the position of executive director followed an independent review of SCPA’s current structure by DeVos Institute of Arts Management based at the University of Maryland. DeVos concluded that, to sustain excellence, additional organizational structure and oversight were warranted given the size and scope of SCPA’s artistic and educational offerings and its ongoing need for fundraising, marketing and community relations.

CPS Superintendent Mary Ronan said her goal is have the executive director begin effective August 1, 2015. Candidates will be interviewed by SCPA’s Local School Decision Making Committee, with recommendations submitted to Ronan. The Cincinnati Board of Education has ultimate authority to approve all hiring decisions.

April 13, 2015

Withrow High Selected as High School of Business Site

Starting in the 2015-16 school year, Withrow University High School will offer rigorous business administration courses through the High School of Business program.

High School of Business is a national program created by MBAResearch, a nonprofit organization specializing in educational research and the development of business and marketing curriculum for high schools and colleges.

The program is designed to challenge high school students with an accelerated, project-based curriculum, aimed at positioning students to excel in college-level business administration programs. When fully implemented, participating students will take a series of six or more rigorous business administration classes. Students also will use technology in each class, complete internships with local businesses, and learn about community and diversity through a partnership with a sister school.

“The High School of Business program will give Withrow’s students opportunities to interact with peers from all over the United States,” Withrow’s Principal Paul Daniels said. “This program also builds relationships with local businesses and universities, and provides an avenue for students who plan to major in accounting, business or marketing to reach higher academic levels."

The first classes offered at Withrow through this program will be Economics and Marketing for students in grades 9-12. Teachers will be offered special training by MBAResearch over four days in June, and will be provided with curriculum, academic material, class projects and methods for interactive learning.

March 25, 2015

PIE Launches First-Ever Android and Apple Apps For Cincinnati Public Schools

Contact: Barbara Grimsley, APR
(513) 325-9086
barbara_e_grimsley@yahoo.com

CINCINNATI – (March 24, 2015) Partnership for Innovation in Education (PIE) will launch the first-ever apps for Android and Apple retail sites for Cincinnati Public Schools on Wednesday, March 25, 2015. The student-designed apps will launch at Kilgour Elementary School at 9:30 a.m. and at Hyde Park School at 1:15 p.m. The apps – “Gelato Hero” and “Sweet Revenge” – feature local businesses and PIE case partners, Madisono’s Gelato and Sorbet and The BonBonerie.

Cincinnati-based PIE works alongside students to develop transformational educational tools to accelerate academic achievement in science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) subjects, especially for girls and minorities. This project features the use of the Socratic Learning Method, which is typically used in graduate and undergraduate programs. It allows business and community executives to serve as in-classroom mentors as students solve a “real life” simulation, which the business is currently facing.

In developing these apps, students from Hyde Park School and Kilgour Elementary School were asked to analyze information, develop research, utilize technology resources and work together to provide workable solutions. With the help of Northern Kentucky University’s Center for Applied Informatics, students learned basic coding and developed interactive, digital apps.

“These apps gameify STEM concepts and offer our local students a chance to experience a 21st century career in a fun, digital format. These games allow students to develop solutions just like an engineer, project director or plant manager. They experience the thrill of ‘doing’ and better understand the meaning of their education,” said Mary Welsh Schlueter, CEO for PIE.

“Gelato Hero”
Students from Kilgour Elementary School designed “Gelato Hero” as part of PIE’s program, and it features Madisono’s Gelato and Sorbet. In this educational game – loosely inspired by the classic video, Berserk – fictional lunch ladies are on a rampage throwing meatballs at people. The “hero” in the game is armed with an unlimited supply of gelato. By throwing the gelato at the lunch ladies, the hero causes them to taste the delicious food and stop their rampaging meatball attack.

“I am truly excited as well as proud of our students and this opportunity for our school. To not only exhibit 21st century skillsets but to turn such skillsets into real word application with the creation of this apps,” said Angela Cook Frazier, principal for Kilgour Elementary School.

“Sweet Revenge”
“Sweet Revenge” was designed by students from Hyde Park School, and it features The BonBonerie. In this classic tower defense game, students must stop an approaching army of ants from eating their cake. Every 10 ants destroyed allows the student to purchase an additional “donut blaster” to stop the ants.

“PIE was a positive collaboration between the community, teachers and students of Hyde Park School. This experience integrated content, and students solved real-world problems,” said Tianay Amat, principal for Hyde Park School.

This project was funded by the Ohio General Assembly and Governor’s Office and facilitated through the Ohio Department of Education. This launch is funded by a “Straight A” Inaugural Grant, consisting of $1.1 million for educational STEM programming with PIE overseeing the work in Cincinnati Public and Milford Exempted Village Schools. “Gelato Hero” and “Sweet Revenge” are only the second set of apps to be released globally in partnership with elementary and middle school students. The first set of PIE apps were launched historically at the Ohio Statehouse with legislators from the Ohio Assembly in February 2015 with the 3M Company and Milford Schools.

About PIE
Partnership For Innovation In Education (PIE) develops transformational educational tools preparing the 21st century workforce for success in the global marketplace. PIE features first-ever mentoring alliances with education, business and arts organizations, providing hands-on simulations in the K-12 classroom. PIE is a 501c3 nonprofit, tax-exempt organization, awarded the GuideStar Nonprofit “Gold Exchange” Ranking. For more information, visit www.piemedia.org or follow @PIE_Innovation on Twitter.

February 10, 2015

Cincinnati Board of Education Names New Treasurer

The Cincinnati Board of Education has unanimously approved a resolution appointing Jennifer M. Wagner as Treasurer and Chief Financial Officer of Cincinnati Public Schools. The action came during the school board’s evening meeting on February 9, 2015.

Wagner, who joined the district in October 1993 and has led various CPS business operations during her tenure, succeeds former Treasurer Diana Whitt, who resigned effective December 31, 2014.

“Jen is a strategic thinker whose familiarity with the district’s business systems and academic priorities position her for great success in directing financial decisions in a way that manages taxpayers’ dollars well,” said Board President Alexander P. Kuhns. “Most importantly, Jen is a servant leader and a role model for administrators in our district.”

Wagner has served as Chief Information Officer (CIO), providing executive oversight to all aspects of information management and technology; Director of Student Information Systems, responsible for student databases; and Director of Total Quality Management, an initiative to infuse total quality management principles and strategic planning into the district. As part of other responsibilities, she has provided executive oversight to the district’s transportation department since December 2011. During transition periods Wagner stepped up twice to fill vacant positions, as Interim Business Executive in 2002 and as Assistant Treasurer/Controller in January 2006.

Prior to joining CPS, Wagner, a Certified Public Accountant, was Vice President/Controller for General Polymers Corp.

“I am thrilled to be afforded this opportunity to serve as Treasurer/CFO for Cincinnati Public Schools,” she said. “This is a perfect blend of my previous professional experiences, merging my accounting background and my more recent Chief Information Officer role.  I am excited to continue my service to the children of Cincinnati in this new capacity.”

Wagner’s position as Treasurer and Chief Financial Officer begins on February 10, 2015. Under Ohio law, school district treasurers, along with school district superintendents, directly report to school boards.

October 21, 2014

New School-Based Health Center Opens on Western Hills High School Campus

The Center is the largest in the region and will provide primary and dental health care.

The new school-based health center will serve the 2,200 students on the Western Hills/Dater High School campus, as well as the surrounding schools and community. Originally implemented with a $375,000 grant from Deaconess Associations Foundation, dental was added when CPS received a $500,000 federal construction grant for the center.

Teens are the most underserved population in the United States for preventive health services. Dental decay is the most common chronic disease of childhood (five times more common than asthma) and 67 percent of permanent teeth have decay by ages 16-19 years. The average high school student misses 2.6 days per year of which 2.3 are due to dental-related issues.

October 1, 2014

Cincinnati Saints, Cincinnati Public Schools, and Activities Beyond the Classroom Form Partnership

The Cincinnati Saints are proud to announce a partnership with Activities Beyond the Classroom (ABC) and Cincinnati Public Schools (CPS). This partnership falls into the Saints' mission of giving back and growing soccer in Cincinnati.

Activities Beyond the Classroom exists to provide support to Cincinnati Public Schools' students for engagement in extracurricular activities throughout the year, offering opportunities to build leadership, teamwork, responsibility and self-discipline to help students succeed in life.

This is a hands-on partnership for the Cincinnati Saints organization. Along with financial support, Saints players and coaches participate in soccer programs brought to CPS by ABC for students in fourth through sixth grades. There currently are 25 CPS elementary schools participating in the girls program. There also is a pilot program for the boys at Roberts Academy in Price Hill.

Cincinnati Public Schools Launches Mobile App to Keep Community Informed and Involved

Cincinnati Public Schools today launched an official mobile app designed to enable parents, students, staff and the broader community to stay informed about important issues, information and activities under way within the school community. August 21, 2014

CPS, Metro Eliminate Extra Bus Fare for Students

Elimination of Cash Drop Designed to Help Families, Attendance

Cincinnati Public Schools (CPS) and Metro are making it easier for students to get to school.

For the 2014-15 school year, due to a revision in the Cincinnati Public Schools policy, students will not have an additional bus fare charge if they use their XTRA Service smart cards. Last year a $.50 cash drop was required. The change applies to CPS students as well as students attending charter and non-public schools within district boundaries who are transported by Metro buses.

August 20, 2014

CPS Launches My Tomorrow*ed Website for New Career Preparation Initiative

Online Tool Provides Clearinghouse of My Tomorrow Resources for CPS Stakeholders

Cincinnati Public Schools today launched the My Tomorrow*ed website, an online information portal that complements the district’s new My Tomorrow initiative to better prepare students to be successful in college and the work force. The new site is located at www.mytomorrowed.org.

My Tomorrow is CPS’s far-reaching plan to reimagine high schools to better prepare students for the careers of their choice. The initiative is rooted in the district’s 2020 Vision that, in six years, a full 100 percent of CPS 7th graders will graduate ready to pursue their chosen career paths.
