 2001 Season Highlights
Over seven figures in official money for the third year in a row, but was the only one of 16 millionaires last year to not win an event...Had a solid campaign that saw him finish among the top 10 in a quarter of his 36 starts...Knocked on the victory door three times only to come up short with three runner-up finishes, all in the second half of the season...Was in the hunt throughout the Instinet Classic, but finished two strokes back of Gil Morgan at the TPC at Jasna Polana...One of only three players in the Novell Utah Showdown field to card three consecutive sub-70 rounds, but came up one stroke shy of Steve Veriato at Park Meadows Country Club...Had victory snatched from his grasp at the Kroger Senior Classic. Closed with a career-best 7-under 63 at Kings Island on Sunday and was two strokes up on Jim Thorpe with one hole to play. Dramatic eagle by Thorpe forced a playoff and then a birdie by Thorpe on the first extra hole proved to be the winner...Was par or better in every second round from the BellSouth Senior Classic at Opryland in early June through the SAS Championship in late September.
 Career Highlights
2000: Parlayed 11 top-10 finishes into a career-best $1.2 million-dollar season...Earned his second career title as a senior at the AT&T Canada Senior Open Championship. Made birdie on the final hole to nip Kermit Zarley by a stroke at St. Charles CC in Winnipeg...Victory came just over a month after his wife, Martha, delivered Tom, Jr...Contended for titles earlier in the year at the Nationwide Championship, BellSouth Senior Classic at Opryland and The Instinet Classic where he was the defending champion. Carded a final-round 69 at the Nationwide, but came up one stroke short of Hale Irwin. Lost out again to Irwin by two strokes in Nashville despite posting a 54-hole score of 16-under 200, his best effort all year in relation to par. Was tied with Bob Murphy for the 36-hole lead at the TPC at Jasna Polana, but dropped back into solo fourth place after shooting a 71 on Sunday...Went over the $2 million in SENIOR TOUR career earnings with his seventh-place finish at the LiquidGolf.com Invitational. 1999: Voted by his peers as the SENIOR TOUR's Comeback Player of the Year...Was third among all senior players with 17 top-10 finishes and won the Bell Atlantic Classic in a playoff near Philadelphia. Defeated Jim Thorpe with a birdie on the first extra hole for his first SENIOR TOUR victory and first TOUR title since the 1975 IVB-Philadelphia Classic. Win at Hartefeld National made him the fifth of a record 11 first-time winners on the season. 1998: Contended for the AT&T Canada Senior Open Championship crown in Calgary, but finished T2 behind Brian Barnes...One of nine players to have two eagles in a round, doing so at the EMC Kaanapali Classic. 1997: Earned a conditional exemption for 1998 by placing 10th at the SENIOR TOUR National Qualifying Tournament in November.
 Personal
Biggest thrills in golf have been playing on the University of Houston's 1970 NCAA Championship team and winning his PGA TOUR title in Philadelphia...A college teammate of John Mahaffey...His family has an involvement in the wine industry in Napa, CA.
