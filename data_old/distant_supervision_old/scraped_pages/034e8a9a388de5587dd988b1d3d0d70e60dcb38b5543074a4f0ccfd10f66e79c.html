Webb’s Reece Britt cards top-10 finish in Division II-A state golf tournament

Posted: Sept. 27, 2016
0 Comments
SHARE

By News Sentinel Staff

Posted: Sept. 27, 2016 0

Webb School's Reece Britt fired his second straight 77 to card a top-10 finish at the Division II-A boys golf state tournament on Tuesday at WillowBrook Golf Club in Manchester.

Britt, an eighth-grader, finished eighth at 10-over-par 154 for the two-day tournament. Winston Margaritis of St. George's was the runaway state champion after shooting even-par 72 to get to 4-under 140 for the tournament. He was the only player to finish under par, besting runner-up Josh Holtschlag (145) by five strokes.

St. George's retained the team title for the second straight year by finishing 48-over 624.

Franklin Road Academy (636) was second, followed by University School of Jackson (639).

Webb, chasing its second championship in three years, was fourth at 664.

Ben Wofford shot 78 for the Spartans on Tuesday, the 10th-best score in the final round. He tied for 15th at 163.

Palmer Bradshaw and Eli Mayes tied for 27th at 174 and Ben Troutman was 30th at 180.

Hannah Powell of St. Andrew's shot 72 on Tuesday to win the girls individual state championship by four strokes. She finished 4-over 148 to surpass first-round leader Victoria England of St. George's.

England finished with a 78 to stumble to 8 over for the tournament.

St. Mary's won the team title at 29-over 317.

FRA (320) was second, followed by USJ (326).

Webb's girls finished sixth at 95-over 383.

The Lady Spartans finished in succession in the individual tournament.

Alexis Hartwig (51-over 193) was 13th, followed by Shelby Bergner (194) and Kalina Scarbrough (195).
