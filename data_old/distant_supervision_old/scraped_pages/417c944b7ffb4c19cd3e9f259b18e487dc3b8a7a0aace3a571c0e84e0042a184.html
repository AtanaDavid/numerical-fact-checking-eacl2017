Strictly Come Dancing 2015: the contestant line-up

  • Rachel Ward
15 December 2015 • 11:45am

All 15 contestants for the 13th series of Strictly are now getting down to business on the dancefloor, but if you still need to get your head around who's who and what's what then we have all the information about the class of 2015 right here.

Kirsty Gallacher - fifth contestant voted off

Kirsty Gallacher, currently a presenter for Sky Sports News, has covered major sporting events such as the Ryder Cup, Wimbledon and the Olympics and has also fronted a number of sporting entertainment shows, including Sky1's Gladiators. She was announced as a contestant on The One Show, shortly before Thomas.

“I’ve always been a huge fan of Strictly so am very excited to be taking part in the show this year," she said.

Kirsty will be dancing with Brendan Cole, who won the glitterball in the very first series.

 

Helen George - tenth contestant voted off

As we reported earlier this month, the Call the Midwife star is indeed joining the Strictly line up. The BBC's official announcement was made at the Edinburgh TV Festival in August.
George said: “I'm really excited to be joining Strictly. I know it's going to be a challenge and if I'm honest I'm slightly terrified, but I can't wait to get my tan and sequins on!”

Helen's professional partner is Aljaž Skorjanec, who won the 2013 series with Abbey Clancy in his debut year.

 

Jamelia - eighth contestant voted off

Jamelia's participation in the show was one of the worst-kept secrets in showbiz: after months of rumours, Jamelia was confirmed as Strictly's 12th contestant.

The Loose Women panellist and former singer, who had eight UK top 10 singles including Superstar and That Boy is Mine, said she was delighted to be taking part, but added that her two daughters are the real dancers in the family: "I definitely want to learn a new skill so that I'll be able to give them a run for their money, and end them teasing me once and for all.”

Jamelia will take to the dancefloor with Irish professional Tristan MacManus, who is in his second year on Strictly.

 

Anita Rani - eleventh contestant voted of

One of those ubiquitous TV faces, Anita Rani has most recently been seen on The World's Busiest Railway 2015. She is also a regular presenter on BBC's Countryfile and The One Show.

"I'm so excited to indulge my fantasy and hopefully learn some mean moves in the process," she says. "Let's get our groove on."

Anita, who won the first series of the BBC’s Great Sport Relief Bake Off, will tango with new pro Gleb Savchenko.

 

Jay McGuiness - winner!

Last year boyband The Wanted went on hiatus to each pursue their own projects, and singer McGuiness is pursuing a different kind of performance for a change.

“My family are such huge Strictly fans, I know I've made them very happy by taking part – I can’t wait to get started," he said.

Jay will be competing for the title with Aliona Vilani, who won the 2011 series with McFly drummer Harry Judd.

 

Anthony Ogogo - second contestant voted off

Sportsman Anthony Ogogo will be swapping the boxing ring for the dancefloor.

"My mum is a massive fan of the show so I will be trying my hardest to make her proud,” said the professional boxer.

Anthony's pro partner is Otile Mabuse, an eight-time South African Latin American champion.

 

Daniel O’Donnell - third contestant voted off

Irish popstar Daniel O'Donnell, who first shot to fame in 1983, was the eighth celebrity to join this year's Strictly line-up.

“Earlier this year, I decided to take a short break from touring and told myself that I would only consider doing something if it was both different and exciting," he said. "Well ,what could be more exciting than Strictly Come Dancing? I love to dance and boy am I looking forward to getting started and being the best that I can be!”

Daniel has been partnered with Siberian siren Kristina Rhianoff.

 

Peter Andre - ninth contestant voted off

Singer and reality TV star Peter Andre was the seventh celebrity contestant to join this year's line-up.

“Strictly Come Dancing is an incredible show," said the Mysterious Girl singer. "I’ve always loved watching it on TV and to be honest I’m flattered to have been asked to take part before, but due to certain work commitments was unable to.”

Peter Andre's mysterious girl is Janette Manrara. They are currently favourites to win.

 

Carol Kirkwood - sixth contestant voted off

BBC weather presenter Carol Kirkwood was the sixth celebrity to cha-cha their way on to the Strictly Come Dancing line-up.

Wearing a white feather boa and maroon dress, she was unveiled on BBC Breakfast.

"It feels really exciting, it doesn't even feel real yet, we haven't started dancing or training or anything yet," she said.

Carol gets the cream of the crop with last year's winner Pasha Kovalev.

 

Katie Derham - fourth place

The broadcaster Katie Derham is swapping the Proms for prime-time television as she signs up for Strictly Come Dancing, a decade after she was "banned" from appearing.

"My boss quite rightly thought I was too busy, there was a lot of pretty serious news going on at the time and it wasn't the sort of thing he wanted me doing," she said, adding the decision was "fair enough".

Katie's partner is Strictly stalwart and all-round gent Anton du Beke.

 

Georgia May Foote - runner-up

The former Coronation Street actress, 24, left her role on the soap earlier this year after four years and announced her involvement in Strictly on BBC Breakfast.

"It's such a relief," she said. "I've known since April, so it's great to be able to tell people."

Georgia, the youngest of the celebrities, is partnered with debut dancer Giovanni, the youngest of the professionals.

 

Kellie Bright - runner-up

The 39-year-old EastEnders actress, who stars on the soap as Queen Vic landlady Linda Carter, revealed the news live on The Chris Evans Show on BBC Radio 2.

“There are two main reasons I want to take part in this year’s Strictly Come Dancing," she said. "The first is that as I will be turning 40 next year, I think it is the perfect time for a new, exciting challenge. The second reason is because if I said ‘no’, Linda Carter would never forgive me!”

The future looks bright for Kellie and she will dance with Kevin "from Grimsby" Clifton who has never been in a dance-off.

 

Ainsley Harriott - fourth contestant voted off

The 58-year-old celebrity chef and former Ready, Steady, Cook host made the announcement on The One Show on BBC One.

“I must admit that I do love to dance, but doing it in front of the nation is a whole different matter," said Harriott. "In my day job the only type of Salsa I know about is the one in my recipes so I am looking forward to rising to the challenge. Bring it on!”

Shakin' it up like Suzy Salt and Percy Pepper, Ainsley dances with towering Australian beauty Natalie Lowe.

 

Jeremy Vine - seventh contestant voted off

The 50-year-old Radio 2 presenter made the announcement on The Chris Evans Breakfast Show on BBC Radio 2 and begin training immediately.

“How could I say no to the biggest show in TV? Yes, yes, yes and yes again to the sequins and the Samba. Now I just need to find some dancing shoes and identify my left foot. I am doing it for my two young daughters who jumped behind the sofa when I told them. It seems Dad on Strictly is more scary than Doctor Who.” he said.

Jeremy's partner is Karen Clifton, who made it to the final last year with Mark Wright.

 

Iwan Thomas - first contestant voted off

Former sprinter Iwan Thomas, who represented Great Britain and Northern Ireland at the Olympic Games in the 400 metres, said: “I’m extremely excited and scared at the thought of taking part in Strictly. I love watching the show."

Iwan will be partnered with Ola Jordan, the programme's longest-standing female pro. The announcement of his inclusion in the show was made live on the BBC's The One Show.
