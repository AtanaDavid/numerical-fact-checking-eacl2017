 NOTES:
      Joe Inman performed a three-peat when  he  captured  last  year's
 event  by  three shots over Larry Nelson. Inman made just one bogey in
 the event, becoming the fifth player in Senior Tour history to win  an
 event three consecutive years. Inman led from start to finish with his
 final round of 65 including five birdies on  the  back  nine.  Inman's
 lone bogey came during the opening round on the fourth hole. Inman and
 Stewart Ginn were the only players in the  field  to  post  all  three
 rounds  in  the  60's.  Ginn finished third, six shots back. Inman has
 posted nine straight rounds in the 60's at this event with  a  scoring
 average  of  66.55.  This is the first of three SBC events on the 2001
 Senior Tour schedule and the eighth of 45 events overall.  Last  year,
 this  event  was  held in October, one week prior to the season-ending
 Tour Championship. Of the  11  previous  tournaments,  six  have  been
 decided  by one shot, one by two shots and two in playoffs. Only twice
 (1992 and 2000) has the event been a runaway, when Raymond  Floyd  won
 by  three  shots and last year. In the events history, no champion has
 ever shot a round over par and only one, Mike Hill has had a round  in
 the  70's  on  his way to victory in 1990. Valencia Country Club takes
 over as the host site for this event. Valencia was  the  site  of  the
 1998  Nissan  Open  on the PGA Tour where Billy Mayfair defeated Tiger
 Woods in a playoff. The event was previously held at Wilshire  Country
 Club  for  the  last  six years while Rancho Park Golf Club hosted the
 tournament the first five years. When Gil Morgan  won  this  event  in
 1996,  he  became  the youngest winner in SENIOR TOUR history, just 11
 days after turning 50. Morgan has won four times  at  three  different
 venues  in Los Angeles - The Glen Campbell Los Angeles Open at Riviera
 Country Club in 1978 and at Rancho Park in 1983, as well as this event
 at Wilshire Country Club in 1996-97. There have been only two playoffs
 in tournament history, the last coming  in  1993  when  Dale  Douglass
 clipped  Jim Dent. Inman will try to become the fifth player in Senior
 Tour history to win the same  event  four  straight  years.  The  four
 players  who  have  accomplished this feat are: Chi Chi Rodriguez, Bob
 Charles, George Archer and Hale Irwin. There have been  six  holes-in-
 one  at  this event. Charles Sifford registered the first one in 1990,
 Jim O'Hern in 1991, Bert Yancey in 1992, Bob Goalby in 1996 and  David
 Graham  and  Bobby  Stroble each had an ace in 1997. Four players have
 participated in all 11 of the previous SBC  Senior  Classics  --  Dale
 Douglass,  Al Geiberger, Rocky Thompson and Tom Shaw. All four players
 are expected to compete this week. Gil Morgan has also enjoyed success
 at  this  event  with two wins and four top-10 finishes in five events
 with a scoring average of 67.87. The Senior Tour moves  north  to  San
 Jose, California for the inaugural Siebel Classic in Silicon Valley.
