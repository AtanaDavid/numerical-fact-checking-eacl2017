Day 8 at the Olympics: What you might have missed on a record-breaking Saturday for GB athletes

Max Miller for Metro.co.ukSunday 14 Aug 2016 4:05 am

Team GB athletes broke records as medals flowed on day eight of the Olympics.

Laura Trott became the first British woman to win three gold medals, and Mo Farah cemented his place as an all-time great by defending his 10,000m title.

Team GB now have 30 medals, with 10 golds, 13 silvers, and 6 bronzes so far.

Here’s what you might have missed on a busy day as the athletics gone underway.

What was the big story for Team GB?

Mo Farah managed to defend his 10,000m Olympic title, blowing away his competitors in yet another brilliant win.

While the 33-year-old won’t be able to celebrate too much with the 5,000m coming up, he was clearly emotional after winning his third gold medal.

He was challenged by Paul Tanui on the final lap, but the Brit’s pace was too much for the Kenyan after the final corner.

Who else won medals?

Kate Archibald, Laura Trott, Elinor Barker and Joanna Rowsell brought home another gold in the cycling, beating the USA in the final of the Women’s Team Pursuit.

This made Trott the first British woman to win three gold medals, and at just 24-years-old she could still win more.

Also in the cycling, Becky James won silver in the Keirin on another successful day in the velodrome.

Earlier in the day, the mens’ eight won gold in the rowing, leading for most of the race in a brilliant performance.

Shortly before, the womens’ eight had grabbed silver after edging out Romania in a photo finish.

While there were hopes of another ‘super Saturday’ to match the three gold medals of London 2012, only Farah was able to grab gold.

Greg Rutherford admitted he was ‘gutted’ afterwards, but still did well to win bronze in an incredible long jump competition. Meanwhile Jessica Ennis-Hill was able to win silver but couldn’t beat Belgian Nafissatou Thiam to gold.

Late on in the day, the men’s 4x100m medley team was able to win silver in the pool, to make it Britain’s most successful Olympics ever in the swimming.

Any shocks?

Nafissatou Thiam admitted that she wasn’t even expected to win a medal in the heptathlon after winning the gold ahead of favourite Jessica Ennis-Hill.

The 21-year-old beat her rivals after an inspired performance with personal bests in the javelin, high jump, and 800m to take gold.

Two time winner Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pryce only won a bronze medal in women’s 100m, losing out to compatriot Elaine Thompson for gold and American Tori Bowie for silver.

What else?

Michael Phelps won gold in what appears to be his final Olympic race, meaning he now has five for Rio 2016 and 28 overall.

Jason Kenny and Callum Skinner both got in to the final of the men’s individual sprint in the cycling, meaning that Team GB are now guaranteed gold and silver in the event.

Meanwhile, there could be even more success for a Brit, with Justin Rose four shots behind leader Marcus Fraser in the golf, with one round left to play.

Also, Juan Martin Del Potro will be Andy Murray’s opponent in the final of the tennis, after he beat Rafael Nadal and the Brit defeated Kei Nishikori.

Something different

Golfer Henrik Stenson took some time to poke an alligator while completing his round, to the shock of many.

The Swede is only two shots off the lead with one round to play, so his supporters may be hoping he doesn’t take any more similar risks while going for a medal.
